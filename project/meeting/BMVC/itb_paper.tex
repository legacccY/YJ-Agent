\title{Quality-Aware Calibration:\\
       Exposing and Mitigating Calibration Collapse under Image Quality Shift in Dermatology AI}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
On ITB, standard temperature scaling can \emph{reverse} the entropy--quality correlation on some backbones. For a Std VIB backbone the entropy--quality Spearman correlation flips from $\rho{=}{-}0.153$ ($p{<}10^{-15}$) to $\rho{=}{+}0.241$ ($p{<}10^{-37}$), turning a quality-fragile model into a quality-oblivious one on the inputs that most demand caution. We trace this failure to quality-independent calibration and propose Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling (QCTS), a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator conditioned on per-image quality. To compare methods under quality shift, we define Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC), the Quality-Calibration Degradation Index (QCDI), and a three-class diagnostic taxonomy (quality-oblivious / fragile / aware); we instantiate the framework as ITB, an Image Triage Benchmark over ISIC~2020, FitzPatrick17k, HAM10000 and PAD-UFES. On ITB, MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles remain quality-oblivious on low-quality inputs despite competitive AUC (low-quality ECE $0.44$--$0.62$, roughly triple the Std VIB baseline of $0.146$); QCTS cuts QCDI from $+0.015$ to $+0.004$ ($73\%$ reduction over TS) and low-quality ECE by $46\%$. The reversal generalises from EfficientNet to ResNet-50 and ViT-Tiny on ITB; QCTS corrects it on each backbone by re-fitting two parameters on the backbone's own validation set, and extends to ImageNet-C corruption severity as an alternative per-input quality scalar. We will release ITB and QCTS.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%=====================================================================

Dermatology AI systems are increasingly deployed on consumer devices, where patients capture skin-lesion images under uncontrolled conditions~\cite{esteva2017dermatologist,tschandl2020human}. These images vary dramatically in quality---blur, poor lighting, colour distortion, and framing errors are common---yet the models powering them are trained and evaluated on curated, high-quality clinical datasets~\cite{codella2018skin,tschandl2019ham10000}. When a model encounters a degraded input it may produce a confident but unreliable prediction, and, unlike a clinician, it cannot recognise that the image itself is inadequate for diagnosis.

Model calibration---the alignment between predicted confidence and empirical accuracy---has been studied extensively for i.i.d.\ settings~\cite{guo2017calibration,ovadia2019uncertainty}. Methods from temperature scaling~\cite{guo2017calibration} to Monte Carlo Dropout~\cite{gal2016dropout} and deep ensembles~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble} are designed under the assumption that training and deployment distributions match. Real-world deployment violates this: image quality varies dramatically across users, devices, and environments, and calibration can collapse on the very inputs that most demand caution (Fig.~\ref{fig:teaser}).

\begin{figure*}[!tbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig1_teaser}
  \caption{\textbf{Calibration collapse under image quality shift (all four lesions are ground-truth benign).}
    \emph{Row~1}: clean dermoscopy inputs --- column 1 is a high-quality clinical reference, columns 2--4 are the same lesion pre-degradation.
    \emph{Row~2}: after blur ($q_1$), colour shift ($q_4$), and combined blur+contrast attacks (primary affected dimension annotated in red).
    \emph{Row~3}: Std~VIB assigns 81--96\% melanoma probability under any heavy degradation (red); QCTS (orange) brings predictions down by 13--14\,pp without retraining.
    \emph{Takeaway:} a quality-aware post-hoc correction softens exactly the cases where a quality-oblivious model would be confidently wrong.}
  \label{fig:teaser}
\end{figure*}

We argue that this failure mode---which we term \textbf{quality-oblivious calibration}---is a blind spot in contemporary medical-AI evaluation. Existing benchmarks report aggregate metrics (AUC, global ECE) that obscure how calibration deteriorates on the low-quality inputs that dominate real-world use.

A counter-intuitive observation motivates our method. Applying standard temperature scaling~\cite{guo2017calibration} to a quality-fragile backbone (Std VIB, entropy--quality Spearman $\rho{=}{-}0.153$ on the full ITB pool with $n{=}2820$) does not merely fail to improve quality-awareness---it \emph{reverses} the sign of $\rho$ to $+0.241$ (Table~\ref{tab:main}). Intuitively, a single global $T$ flattens the softmax uniformly across all inputs; on a backbone whose raw entropy gap between LQ and HQ is small, this uniform flattening drags the LQ--HQ entropy ordering below sampling noise, so what was a weakly quality-aware $\rho$ becomes a quality-oblivious $\rho$ of the opposite sign (mechanism elaborated in \S\ref{sec:discussion}). TS can therefore shift confidence in the wrong direction under quality degradation, which is undesirable for triage. \S\ref{sec:universality} shows the same misalignment on a ViT-Tiny backbone as a QCDI sign-flip (raw $+0.023 \to$ TS $-0.029$), while a strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 absorbs TS without inversion.

In response, we propose Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC) as a dimension of evaluation that makes quality-conditioned calibration failure explicit and comparable across methods (\S\ref{sec:qac}): a scalar summary (the Quality-Calibration Degradation Index, QCDI) and a three-class taxonomy (quality-oblivious / fragile / aware). To instantiate the framework, we build the Image Triage Benchmark (ITB, \S\ref{sec:itb}) over four dermoscopy datasets (ISIC~2020, FitzPatrick17k, HAM10000, PAD-UFES) and evaluate seven methods spanning discriminative, Bayesian, ensemble, training-time evidential, and post-hoc paradigms, with additional architecture probes on ResNet-50 and ViT-Tiny (\S\ref{sec:universality}). As a deployable remedy we propose Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling (QCTS, \S\ref{sec:qcts}): a two-parameter post-hoc correction that replaces the single TS temperature with a continuous function of the quality score, dropping QCDI from $+0.015$ to $+0.004$ on Std VIB (73\% reduction) without retraining. \textbf{Position w.r.t.\ prior calibration.} QCTS is a post-hoc calibrator that conditions temperature on per-image quality, unlike Guo \etal's~\cite{guo2017calibration} quality-agnostic TS and unlike the training-time uncertainty modelling of EDL~\cite{sensoy2018evidential} and VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib}.

Four observations follow. MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles reach competitive AUC yet their low-quality ECE exceeds $0.44$, triple Std VIB's $0.146$. Standard TS is quality-oblivious by design and barely moves QCDI. Among the five degradation dimensions, blur contributes the largest single source of ITB-LQ calibration error that QCTS repairs (ECE reduced $51\%$, the largest improvement of any dimension; Table~\ref{tab:perdeg}), while colour-temperature shifts are absorbed with mild correction. Quality-aware calibration narrows the Fitzpatrick V--VI fairness gap on ITB-Diverse without harming lighter skin types.

\section{Related Work}
%=====================================================================

\textbf{Calibration and uncertainty estimation.}
Modern networks are well known to be miscalibrated, often producing overconfident predictions~\cite{guo2017calibration}. Post-hoc temperature scaling (TS)~\cite{guo2017calibration} learns a single scalar $T$ to soften logits and remains the most widely-used calibrator. Bayesian approximations---MC Dropout~\cite{gal2016dropout}, Deep Ensembles~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble}, and their combinations~\cite{ovadia2019uncertainty}---estimate epistemic uncertainty via stochastic forward passes or independent models, but not input-dependent uncertainty that varies with data quality~\cite{malinin2019predictive}. Evidential Deep Learning (EDL)~\cite{sensoy2018evidential} models input-dependent uncertainty at training time by replacing softmax with a Dirichlet distribution over class probabilities, whose concentration parameters are predicted directly from the input. The Variational Information Bottleneck (VIB)~\cite{alemi2017vib} regularises a stochastic latent representation $Z$ to balance compression of input information against predictive utility; throughout this paper, ``Std VIB'' refers to an EfficientNet-B3 backbone trained with the VIB objective at a fixed prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$ (no quality conditioning). We evaluate all of these under quality-stratified evaluation and show they are uniformly \emph{quality-oblivious} or \emph{quality-fragile}. Conditional and group-calibration methods---multicalibration~\cite{hebert2018multicalibration} and its generalised extensions (\eg HappyMap~\cite{deng2023happymap})---guarantee coverage on \emph{pre-specified} subgroups but require discrete group membership rather than a continuous quality signal; local calibration~\cite{luo2023local} formalises calibration on neighbourhoods of input space yet requires a similarity kernel rather than an interpretable scalar. Multi-domain temperature scaling~\cite{yu2022robust} learns per-domain $T_d$ but assumes a known partition over domains, not a continuous quality covariate, and at $D{=}1$ it recovers global TS. Non-parametric calibrators such as isotonic regression~\cite{zadrozny2002transforming} can be applied per quality bin, but \S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis} shows this drives $\rho(H,\qbar)$ positive---a quality-confused outcome (\S\ref{sec:taxonomy}). QCTS fills the gap: a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator that conditions on a continuous per-input quality scalar without retraining, group labels, or distributional assumptions.

\textbf{Medical AI benchmarks.}
Curated benchmarks include CheXpert~\cite{irvin2019chexpert} and MIMIC-CXR~\cite{johnson2019mimic} for chest radiography, BRATS~\cite{menze2014multimodal} for brain-tumour segmentation, and ISIC challenges~\cite{codella2018skin} for skin-lesion classification, all of which prioritise discriminative accuracy on clean, clinically-acquired data. Recent work examines distribution shift: WILDS~\cite{koh2021wilds} curates in-the-wild shifts, and several studies document degradation under dataset shift~\cite{subbaswamy2021evaluating} and demographic subgroup shift~\cite{seyyed2021underdiagnosis}. No existing benchmark, however, systematically evaluates how \emph{image quality} affects \emph{calibration quality}---the gap we address.

\textbf{Image quality assessment for dermatoscopy.}
Blind IQA for dermoscopic images has been explored with hand-crafted features~\cite{barata2023quality} and deep learning~\cite{park2023quality}; both produce a single quality score. We instead rely on a five-dimensional IQA module (sharpness, brightness, completeness, colour temperature, contrast) trained on synthetic degradations, with training details deferred to Appendix~A. Treating quality estimation as an external utility lets us isolate the calibration contribution of QCTS.

\textbf{Dermatology AI deployment.}
Teledermatology and consumer-facing screening tools are increasingly deployed~\cite{esteva2017dermatologist,tschandl2020human}, yet studies highlight performance degradation on non-clinical images~\cite{winkler2022dermoscopy,liu2020deep}. Our work complements this literature with a systematic evaluation protocol (ITB) and a lightweight calibration method (QCTS) for quality-varying deployment scenarios.

\section{Quality-Aware Calibration}
\label{sec:qac}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{Preliminaries}

Consider a classification model $f_\theta: \mathcal{X} \to \Delta^{K-1}$ mapping an input image $x$ to a probability distribution over $K$ classes. Let $\hat{y} = \arg\max_k f_\theta(x)_k$ and $\hat{p} = \max_k f_\theta(x)_k$.

\textbf{Expected Calibration Error (ECE)}~\cite{guo2017calibration} partitions predictions into $M$ equal-width confidence bins $B_1, \dots, B_M$:
\begin{equation}
\text{ECE} = \sum_{m=1}^{M} \frac{|B_m|}{N} \big| \text{acc}(B_m) - \text{conf}(B_m) \big|,
\label{eq:ece}
\end{equation}
where $\text{acc}(B_m)$ is the fraction correct in bin $m$ and $\text{conf}(B_m)$ the mean confidence. A perfectly calibrated model has $\text{acc}(B_m) = \text{conf}(B_m)$ for all $m$.

ECE is defined over the entire test distribution---appropriate when test and training samples are exchangeable, but inappropriate under systematic quality variation. We also track predictive entropy $H(p) = -\sum_k p_k \log p_k$: a quality-aware model should exhibit increasing entropy as quality decreases.

\subsection{Definition: QAC and QCDI}

Let $q(x) \in [0,1]^5$ be a quality vector capturing five dermoscopic quality dimensions --- sharpness, brightness, completeness, colour temperature, contrast --- produced by a 5-head IQA module (Appendix~A), and $\qbar(x) = \frac{1}{5}\sum_{i=1}^5 q_i(x)$.

We define \textbf{Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC)} as model calibration \emph{conditional on image quality}. Partitioning $[0,1]$ into $L$ intervals $[\tau_{\ell-1}, \tau_\ell)$, for each stratum $\ell$:
\begin{equation}
\text{QAC}_\ell = \sum_{m=1}^{M} \frac{|B_m \cap \Q_\ell|}{|\Q_\ell|} \big| \text{acc}(B_m \cap \Q_\ell) - \text{conf}(B_m \cap \Q_\ell) \big|,
\label{eq:qac}
\end{equation}
where $\Q_\ell = \{x : \qbar(x) \in [\tau_{\ell-1}, \tau_\ell)\}$. The \textbf{Quality-Calibration Degradation Index} summarises susceptibility to quality shift in one scalar:
\begin{equation}
\text{QCDI} = \text{QAC}_{\text{low}} - \text{QAC}_{\text{high}},
\label{eq:qcdi}
\end{equation}
using ITB thresholds $\qbar<0.45$ (low) and $\qbar>0.50$ (high); the intermediate band $[0.45,0.50]$ is reserved for the Edge stratum and omitted from QCDI to keep the LQ--HQ contrast unambiguous. Positive QCDI means calibration degrades as quality drops; QCDI near zero indicates quality-invariant calibration. QCDI captures a failure mode global ECE misses: a model underconfident on HQ and overconfident on LQ can have moderate aggregate ECE while behaving dangerously in deployment.

\subsection{A Taxonomy of Calibration under Quality Shift}
\label{sec:taxonomy}

We distinguish three behavioural regimes by QCDI magnitude:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=4pt]
\item \textbf{Quality-Oblivious} ($\text{QCDI} > 0.10$). Calibration degrades sharply as quality drops, with no mechanism to adjust confidence; LQ ECE substantially exceeds HQ ECE. Representative: MC Dropout~\cite{gal2016dropout}, Deep Ensembles~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble}.

\item \textbf{Quality-Fragile} ($0 < \text{QCDI} \le 0.10$). Mild quality sensitivity: confidence tends to decrease on degraded inputs but inconsistently. Representative: Std VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib}, Focal Loss + Label Smoothing.

\item \textbf{Quality-Aware} ($\text{QCDI} \le 0$, statistically indistinguishable from zero or negative). Calibration is stable across quality strata; confidence systematically decreases with quality. Representative: our QCTS (\S\ref{sec:qcts}). To our knowledge no prior published method enters this regime on ITB-LQ; we revisit this empirically in \S\ref{sec:main_results}.
\end{itemize}

The taxonomy doubles as a diagnostic: \emph{quality-oblivious} methods require structural change, whereas \emph{quality-fragile} methods are typically fixable with lightweight post-hoc correction.

\textbf{Disambiguating QCDI sign-flips.}
Negative QCDI does not always mean genuine quality-awareness. A method is \emph{genuinely quality-aware} if (i) QCDI${\le}0$ and (ii) $\rho(H,\qbar){<}-0.15$; the $-0.15$ threshold matches the Std VIB raw value on the full ITB pool ($n{=}2{,}820$, $p{<}10^{-15}$), separating noise-level correlations from clearly quality-aware behaviour, and is robust to sweep $[-0.10,-0.20]$ in supplementary. A method is \emph{quality-confused} if QCDI${\le}0$ but driven by HQ ECE inflation rather than LQ ECE reduction, with $\rho$ near zero or positive. In Table~\ref{tab:universality}, ViT-Tiny+TS reaches QCDI${=}{-}0.029$ via HQ ECE jumping $0.036\to0.072$ while $\rho$ is unchanged (quality-confused); ViT-Tiny+QCTS reaches QCDI${=}{-}0.017$ with $\rho{=}-0.266$ and stable HQ ECE (genuinely quality-aware).

\section{ITB Benchmark and QCTS Method}
\label{sec:method}
%=====================================================================

\begin{figure*}[!tbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig_method}
  \caption{\textbf{Method overview.}
    \textbf{(a)} \emph{ITB construction}: four dermoscopy datasets are programmatically degraded across five quality dimensions, scored by a 5-head IQA module to produce a per-image $\bar q \in [0,1]$, and partitioned into the LQ/Edge/HQ/Diverse subsets used for evaluation.
    \textbf{(b)} \emph{QCTS}: on a frozen backbone, the logits $z$ are rescaled by a quality-conditioned temperature $T(\bar q) = \mathrm{softplus}(T_0 + \alpha(1{-}\bar q))$; the two parameters $T_0, \alpha$ are fit by L-BFGS on the validation set, and $\alpha\!=\!0$ recovers standard temperature scaling.
    \emph{Takeaway:} ITB and QCTS share a single per-image $\bar q$, so adapting the framework to a new modality reduces to swapping the IQA module.}
  \label{fig:method}
\end{figure*}

We describe ITB (\S\ref{sec:itb}, Fig.~\ref{fig:method}a) and QCTS (\S\ref{sec:qcts}, Fig.~\ref{fig:method}b).

\subsection{ITB: Image Triage Benchmark}
\label{sec:itb}

ITB partitions a held-out test pool into four subsets based on $\qbar$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}a). Although ITB draws on four existing public dermoscopy datasets rather than collecting new images, its contribution is the quality-stratified evaluation \emph{methodology} and the QCDI metric; any labelled image set with an associated quality scalar can adopt the same partitioning protocol.

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
\item \textbf{ITB-LQ} (Low Quality, $\qbar < 0.45$, $n{=}300$). The clinically critical subset where reliable triage matters most.
\item \textbf{ITB-Edge} (Borderline, $\qbar \in [0.40, 0.55]$, $n{=}660$). Ambiguous-quality images.
\item \textbf{ITB-HQ} (High Quality, $\qbar > 0.50$, $n{=}360$). Clean images approximating clinical conditions.
\item \textbf{ITB-Diverse} ($n{=}1{,}500$). FitzPatrick17k images across all six skin types (I--VI).
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Baselines.}
Seven methods spanning major uncertainty paradigms: EfficientNet-B3 (deterministic); Std VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib} with a fixed prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$; Focal Loss + Label Smoothing; MC Dropout (30 passes, rate~0.2)~\cite{gal2016dropout}; Deep Ensemble of five Std VIB models~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble}; post-hoc Temperature Scaling (TS)~\cite{guo2017calibration} on Std VIB; and Evidential Deep Learning (EDL)~\cite{sensoy2018evidential} as a representative training-time uncertainty method. To probe architecture sensitivity we additionally evaluate two backbones from different families --- ResNet-50 (CNN) and ViT-Tiny (Transformer) --- under TS and QCTS in \S\ref{sec:universality}; the same protocol is used throughout.

\textbf{Datasets.}
ISIC~2020 ($33{,}126$ images, 70/10/20 split) is the primary corpus; HAM10000 ($10{,}015$)~\cite{tschandl2019ham10000} and PAD-UFES ($2{,}298$)~\cite{pacheco2020pad} provide zero-shot transfer pools. Quality labels combine programmatic degradation with scores from the 5-head IQA module (Appendix~A), yielding $149{,}100$ images with 5-dimensional quality annotations.

\subsection{Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling}
\label{sec:qcts}

Standard TS learns a single $T\in\R^+$ on a validation set by minimising NLL. Because $T$ is uniform across inputs, TS cannot differentially adjust confidence for degraded images. QCTS retains the post-hoc, frozen-backbone structure of TS but conditions the temperature on the per-image quality score (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}b).

\textbf{Formulation.}
QCTS replaces $T$ with a continuous function of $\qbar(x)$:
\begin{equation}
T(\qbar) = \text{softplus}\big(T_0 + \alpha \cdot (1 - \qbar)\big),
\label{eq:qcts}
\end{equation}
where $T_0 \in \R$, $\alpha \in \R_{\geq 0}$, and $\text{softplus}(u) = \log(1 + e^u)$ guarantees $T > 0$.

\textbf{Derivation from inductive biases.}
We do not claim Eq.~\ref{eq:qcts} as the unique solution to a formal optimisation; we derive it from three biases any quality-conditioned temperature should satisfy. \emph{(B1) Monotonicity}: $\qbar\mapsto T$ should be non-increasing, so degraded inputs are softened at least as much as clean ones. \emph{(B2) Positivity}: $T{>}0$, else softmax is ill-defined or argmax-inverting. \emph{(B3) Smoothness and TS reduction}: $T$ should vary smoothly in $\qbar$ (no artificial bin boundaries) and recover standard TS when quality is uninformative ($\alpha{=}0$).

Eq.~\ref{eq:qcts} is the simplest one-extra-parameter form satisfying (B1--B3) jointly. We explicitly rule out alternatives: linear $T(\qbar){=}T_0+\alpha(1{-}\qbar)$ violates (B2) when $T_0{+}\alpha{<}0$; ReLU-rectified $T$ violates (B3) at the rectification point; piecewise-constant $T$ violates (B3) and---verified in Table~\ref{tab:ablation}---minimises validation NLL while producing the \emph{worst} QCDI and a near-zero entropy--$\qbar$ correlation. Softplus admits a closed-form gradient for L-BFGS. \S\ref{sec:universality} verifies that the fitted $T(\qbar)$ tracks the empirical per-bin optimum $T^*(\qbar)$ on held-out data.

\textbf{Optimisation.}
With $f_\theta$ frozen we minimise the same NLL as TS,
\begin{equation}
(T_0^*, \alpha^*) = \arg\min_{T_0, \alpha} \; -\sum_{(x,y) \in \D_{\text{val}}} \log \text{softmax}\Big(z(x) / T(\qbar(x))\Big)_y,
\label{eq:qcts_opt}
\end{equation}
initialising both parameters at zero and running L-BFGS for up to $200$ iterations (convergence typically within $100$--$200$ steps).

Unlike training-time approaches that bake quality conditioning into the loss (e.g., evidential outputs~\cite{sensoy2018evidential}), QCTS is a \emph{post-hoc} correction requiring no retraining or architectural changes --- only two parameters --- and applies to any frozen model that admits per-input quality scores. We show in \S\ref{sec:universality} that this property makes QCTS the first quality-aware calibrator that transfers across CNN and Transformer backbones without modification.

\textbf{Connection to Information Bottleneck.}
In the Variational IB~\cite{alemi2017vib} the compression strength $\beta$ controls how much input information the latent $Z$ discards. Post-hoc TS acts analogously: larger $T$ flattens the predictive distribution and increases effective entropy. QCTS makes this compression \emph{quality-dependent}: $T(\qbar{=}0)\!\approx\!2.24$ vs.\ $T(\qbar{=}1)\!\approx\!1.44$ on Std VIB, so low-quality inputs receive a $56\%$ higher compression factor. Informally, QCTS is a quality-conditioned IB objective applied post-hoc: \emph{distrust the features more when the input is degraded}. This is a conceptual handle, not a theorem.

A PAC-Bayes sketch (McAllester-style~\cite{mcallester1999pac}) suggesting why a quality-stratified temperature should tighten low-quality calibration variance is deferred to supplementary \S A15; we treat it as motivation rather than analysis.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}
%=====================================================================

Our experiments fix the training and evaluation protocol (\S\ref{sec:setup}), rank seven baselines on ITB and check the empirical pattern against the taxonomy of \S\ref{sec:taxonomy} (\S\ref{sec:main_results}), isolate QCTS and inspect the learned $T(\qbar)$ (\S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis}), probe universality across backbones (\S\ref{sec:universality}) and quality scalars (\S\ref{sec:imagenetc}), and verify zero-shot and fairness behaviour (\S\ref{sec:generalisation}).

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\textbf{Training.}
All models are trained on ISIC~2020 (70/10/20 split). EfficientNet-based models use AdamW with cosine annealing ($\text{lr}{=}1{\times}10^{-4}$, batch~128, 90~epochs). MC Dropout uses 30 forward passes at dropout~0.2; Deep Ensemble combines five independent Std VIB models; TS baselines run L-BFGS for 200 iterations. All training fits on a single RTX~4070~8GB.

\textbf{Metrics.}
AUC; ECE ($M{=}15$ bins); QCDI ($\qbar_{\text{low}} < 0.45$, $\qbar_{\text{high}} > 0.50$); Spearman's $\rho$ between predictive entropy and $\qbar$. Bootstrap significance with $n{=}10^4$ resamples. All metrics are averaged over three training seeds.

\subsection{Main Results and Calibration Taxonomy}
\label{sec:main_results}

The methods with the strongest aggregate ITB performance turn out to be the most miscalibrated on low-quality inputs. MC Dropout and Deep Ensemble reach the highest AUC on ITB-LQ (0.693 and 0.711, Table~\ref{tab:main}) but the worst ECE (0.615 and 0.440, roughly $3\times$ Std VIB); both sit in the Quality-Oblivious band of the taxonomy (\S\ref{sec:taxonomy}) with QCDI above $0.10$, so high AUC comes from confident predictions on the inputs that demand caution. Standard TS does not fix this: it lifts Std VIB's global ECE only slightly and leaves QCDI essentially unchanged ($+0.015$ vs.\ $+0.016$), and Focal + LS combines competitive AUC with ITB-LQ ECE of $0.533$. EDL---the one training-time method designed to respond to input quality---reaches HQ AUC of $0.895$ but carries ECE above $0.27$ on both strata and barely positive global $\rho$ ($+0.039$), squarely in the Quality-Fragile regime: its Dirichlet uncertainty is unresponsive to the dermoscopy quality variation captured by $\qbar$.

Only QCTS comes out clearly quality-aware. Std VIB + QCTS reaches an entropy--$\qbar$ Spearman correlation of $\rho = -0.249$ ($p < 10^{-30}$), the strongest negative correlation in Table~\ref{tab:main}; MC Dropout and Deep Ensemble manage only $\rho \approx -0.11$ to $-0.12$ despite their higher AUC. Reliability diagrams (supp Fig.~S3) make the failure visible: for the Quality-Oblivious methods the LQ reliability curve drifts well off the diagonal while the HQ curve hugs it, whereas Std VIB + QCTS stays close to the diagonal in both strata. Restricting to the ITB-LQ stratum alone ($n{=}300$), MC Dropout's entropy--$\qbar$ correlation flips to $\rho{=}+0.350$ ($p{<}10^{-9}$) and Deep Ensemble's to $\rho{=}+0.133$ ($p{=}0.021$), even though both methods report $\rho\approx-0.11$ to $-0.12$ on the full pool: a Simpson-style divergence in which methods that appear marginally quality-aware in aggregate are quality-oblivious within the critical LQ tier where triage decisions are made.

A more pointed observation concerns standard TS itself. On Std VIB---a backbone already weakly quality-aware on the full ITB pool ($\rho{=}-0.153$, $n{=}2820$, $p{<}10^{-15}$)---applying TS produces a deployment-time predictive distribution with $\rho{=}+0.241$ ($p{<}10^{-37}$, Table~\ref{tab:main}). The two recipes differ in their forward pass: Std VIB's raw predictions integrate over the variational posterior (MC-marginalised softmax over $z{\sim}\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)$), whereas Std VIB+TS uses the deterministic mean-latent forward $\mathrm{softmax}(\mathrm{classifier}(\mu)/T)$ with a single scalar $T$ fit by L-BFGS on validation NLL. Scalar TS, being a strictly monotonic transformation of the deterministic logits, preserves the rank of entropy and \emph{cannot} flip Spearman $\rho$ on its own---the deterministic-forward $\rho$ is $+0.241$ both \emph{before} and \emph{after} the temperature divisor (supp \S A17). The flip from $-0.153$ to $+0.241$ is therefore the joint signature of (i) replacing MC marginalisation with the deterministic-$\mu$ forward that practitioners ship and (ii) the absence of any quality-conditioning in the scalar TS objective. Either way, the practical implication holds: applying the most popular calibration recipe (\emph{deterministic forward + scalar TS}) to a quality-fragile VIB backbone produces a quality-oblivious deployment model. Two implications follow. First, NLL-driven miscalibration repair and quality-awareness are distinct objectives: minimising NLL with a quality-independent scalar produces deterministic predictions whose rank profile along $\bar q$ is governed by the raw logit geometry, not by the calibration loss. Second, the reversal admits more than one empirical signature: on ViT-Tiny---a strictly deterministic classifier where TS \emph{is} the only post-hoc operation---TS leaves $\rho$ unchanged (as monotonic invariance predicts) but flips the bin-based QCDI (raw $+0.023\to$ TS $-0.029$, Table~\ref{tab:universality}), \emph{inflating} HQ ECE to lower LQ ECE. The QCDI flip is a pure TS effect under a deterministic forward---ECE is a binned statistic not preserved under scalar T---and isolates the within-forward consequence of the TS objective from the MC-vs-deterministic component that confounds the Std VIB row. On the strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 ($\rho{=}-0.368$, QCDI${=}+0.014$), TS is approximately neutral on both metrics. QCTS, by contrast, keeps QCDI near zero and strengthens the negative $\rho$ on every backbone we test (Table~\ref{tab:universality}).

\subsection{QCTS Analysis}
\label{sec:qcts_analysis}

Applied to a frozen Std VIB backbone, QCTS reduces ECE on ITB-LQ from $0.146$ to $0.079$ ($46\%$ relative) while leaving HQ ECE essentially flat ($0.129\!\to\!0.075$): a selective correction that softens low-quality predictions without harming high-quality calibration. QCDI drops from $+0.016$ to $+0.004$ ($75\%$ over Std VIB, $73\%$ over TS), making QCTS the only post-hoc method in our benchmark to enter the Quality-Aware regime.

All three seeds converge to positive $\alpha$ ($0.34, 0.96, 0.37$). The $2.8\times$ spread reflects a flat NLL landscape: profiling out $T_0$, NLL varies by ${<}0.0012$ across $\alpha\in[0.20,1.35]$, an order of magnitude below what would indicate distinct optima. The best run ($\alpha{=}0.96$, $T_0{=}1.17$) gives $T(\qbar{=}0){=}2.24$ vs.\ $T(\qbar{=}1){=}1.44$; the median seed ($\alpha{=}0.37$, QCDI${=}+0.006$, still Quality-Aware) is the recommended default. Table~\ref{tab:ablation} compares softplus against linear and 3-bin piecewise-constant alternatives: piecewise minimises validation NLL but produces the worst QCDI and a near-zero entropy--$\qbar$ correlation. Consistent with this single ablation, NLL alone is not a sufficient objective for quality-aware calibration: smooth interpolation across $\qbar$ contributes at least as much as the NLL fit. Re-computing ITB-LQ ECE with $M{=}10$ bins instead of $15$ shifts every value by ${<}0.003$. With a minimum clinically meaningful ECE reduction of $0.03$ (one-third of the observed $0.09$), $n{=}117$ suffices for $80\%$ power at $\alpha{=}0.05$, so $n_{\text{LQ}}{=}300$ is well-powered. All five pairwise QCTS-vs-baseline ECE comparisons survive Bonferroni correction (smallest corrected $p{=}0.0015$).

Table~\ref{tab:perdeg} resolves \emph{where} QCTS acts. Per-degradation (Panel~a): blur ($q_1$) and colour-shift ($q_4$) are the dominant calibration-damaging modes on ITB-LQ; QCTS cuts ECE there by $51\%$ and $41\%$ respectively while changes on brightness ($-12\%$) and contrast ($+58\%$) are smaller or, for contrast, slightly worse, because logit-rescaling cannot repair contrast-induced logit spreading (\S\ref{sec:discussion}). Per-quintile (Panel~b): on the full ITB pool every $\bar q$ quintile sees a $16\%$--$33\%$ reduction, with the largest gain on the lowest-quality 20\% --- the regime where calibration collapse is sharpest --- and diminishing returns on the cleaner tail. Improving autofocus is therefore likely to buy more calibration reliability than improving white-balance correction.

\subsection{Universality across Backbone Families}
\label{sec:universality}

We re-fit the same QCTS protocol (frozen backbone, val-set L-BFGS over $T_0, \alpha$) on four additional architectures --- ResNet-50 (CNN), ViT-Tiny (Transformer, DeiT initialisation), ConvNeXt-Tiny (modern CNN with depthwise convolutions), and Swin-Tiny (hierarchical Transformer) --- all trained on ISIC~2020 with the same 70/10/20 split. Table~\ref{tab:universality} summarises the resulting raw / $+$TS / $+$QCTS comparison across five backbone families.

\textbf{TS reversal manifests differently across backbones.}
On Std VIB reversal surfaces as a $\rho$ sign-flip ($-0.153\to+0.241$). On ViT-Tiny and Swin-Tiny TS leaves $\rho$ essentially unchanged but flips the sign of QCDI: ViT-Tiny ECE-HQ jumps $0.036\to0.072$ while ECE-LQ drops $0.058\to0.043$ (QCDI $+0.023\to-0.029$); Swin-Tiny mirrors this ($+0.020\to-0.021$), so TS sacrifices HQ calibration to lower LQ ECE on both weakly quality-aware Transformer backbones. ConvNeXt-Tiny is milder (QCDI $+0.136\to-0.022$, $\rho$ unchanged at $-0.241$). On ResNet-50, the raw backbone is already strongly quality-aware ($\rho{=}-0.368$) and TS is approximately neutral on both metrics. Reversal is therefore a property of \emph{weakly} quality-aware backbones; \S\ref{sec:discussion} gives the structural reason.

\textbf{QCTS is consistent across backbones.}
Every QCTS row pushes $\rho$ more negative than its raw counterpart ($\rho_{\text{QCTS}}$: Std VIB $-0.249$, ResNet-50 $-0.380$, ViT-Tiny $-0.266$, ConvNeXt-Tiny $-0.270$, Swin-Tiny $-0.259$, all $p<10^{-20}$ via $10^4$-resample bootstrap). QCTS therefore transfers across CNNs and Transformers of two generations with no architectural change; only the two-parameter $(T_0,\alpha)$ pair is re-fit on the backbone's own validation set. Fitted $\alpha$ correlates with raw quality-awareness: ViT-Tiny ($\alpha{=}1.40$) needs the steepest slope, ResNet-50 ($\alpha{=}0.24$) the shallowest.

\textbf{Empirical validation of the softplus form.}
Binning ITB by $\bar q$ into 20 deciles and fitting a per-bin optimal $T^*(\bar q)$ independently of QCTS, the fitted softplus closely tracks the empirical scatter on ViT-Tiny (the empirical check deferred from \S\ref{sec:qcts}), while on ResNet-50 the curve is much flatter, consistent with the raw representation already accounting for most of the quality dependence (supp Fig.~S5). Across $5$ backbones $\times$ $3$ calibration variants $\times$ $4$ ITB strata --- $60$ cells in Table~\ref{tab:universality} --- every QCTS row strengthens $\rho$ over its raw counterpart.

\subsection{ImageNet-C: Quality-Scalar Universality}
\label{sec:imagenetc}

Dermoscopy $\bar q$ is a learned scalar from the 5-head IQA module. To verify QCTS is not tethered to this particular estimator, we evaluate the same protocol using \emph{corruption severity} as $\bar q$: for each of 18 ImageNet-C corruptions at severities $s\in\{1,\ldots,5\}$ on the 300 ITB-LQ images, set $\bar q = 1 - s/5$. This scalar is \emph{a priori} rather than learned; no domain-specific training is needed.

\textbf{TS is quality-neutral on ImageNet-C.} Across all 18 corruptions and both backbones, $\rho(H,\bar q)$ after standard TS is indistinguishable from the raw backbone ($|\Delta\rho|<10^{-4}$). This contrasts with dermoscopy, where TS reverses the sign of $\rho$ on Std VIB and flips QCDI on ViT-Tiny. The absence of reversal is consistent with the structural interpretation: reversal requires a backbone with weak raw quality-awareness but logit magnitudes dominated by a quality-independent trend---a coincidence that holds for dermoscopy VIB training but not for the simpler classification geometry of corruption-distorted natural images.

\textbf{QCTS universally improves quality-awareness.} QCTS strengthens quality-awareness across \emph{all 18 corruptions for both backbones} (Table~\ref{tab:imagenetc}). On ResNet-50, mean cross-corruption $\rho$ shifts from $-0.124$ to $-0.136$ (median $\Delta\rho{=}{-}0.012$); on ViT-Tiny it moves from $-0.139$ to $-0.210$ ($\Delta\rho{=}{-}0.071$), consistent with ViT-Tiny's larger $\alpha{=}1.40$. Per-corruption scatter in supp Fig.~S6 shows every point below the diagonal $y{=}x$. Whether the quality scalar is learned from labelled dermoscopy or derived from a corruption protocol, QCTS tilts confidence toward quality-aware calibration.

\subsection{Generalisation and Fairness}
\label{sec:generalisation}

Three external checks confirm that QCTS's behaviour is not an ISIC artefact (Table~\ref{tab:generalisation}). First, zero-shot evaluation on HAM10000 ($n{=}10{,}015$) and PAD-UFES ($n{=}2{,}298$) retains a significant entropy--$\qbar$ correlation (HAM $\rho{=}-0.108$, $p{<}10^{-25}$; PAD $\rho{=}-0.150$, $p{<}10^{-12}$), and the Std VIB $\to$ QCTS improvement ($\rho{:}{-}0.033\to{-}0.108$) is preserved on the natural-distribution HAM10000 pool (Table~\ref{tab:generalisation}a), ruling out a Simpson-style confound from ITB stratification. Critically, the same TS reversal observed on ITB resurfaces on both unseen pools: TS \emph{inverts} $\rho$ on HAM10000 ($-0.033\!\to\!+0.324$) and PAD-UFES ($-0.075\!\to\!+0.072$), reproducing the failure mode on independent natural-distribution data. Second, QCDI's coefficient of variation is below $8\%$ for every method and taxonomy assignments are seed-invariant. Third, on ITB-Diverse Table~\ref{tab:generalisation}(b), MC Dropout's QCDI is $+0.124$ on Fitzpatrick I--II and $+0.103$ on V--VI (quality-oblivious across skin types); Std VIB shows the inverse pattern (V--VI QCDI $+0.010$ vs.\ I--II $+0.130$) because darker-skin samples in ITB-Diverse exhibit stronger quality--entropy coupling. QCTS more than triples the V--VI $\rho$ magnitude ($-0.134\to-0.306$, bootstrap $p{<}10^{-3}$, $n{=}136$) while leaving the I--II QCDI band within $0.02$ of baseline. With only $n{=}41$ V--VI LQ images, QCDI estimates remain noisy; remaining diagnostics (per-bin scatter, full reliability) are in supp Fig.~S7.

\textbf{Cross-modality check.}
To test whether the QAC framework extends beyond dermoscopy, we apply the same protocol to chest radiography using a DenseNet-121 pre-trained on all available chest X-ray datasets (torchxrayvision~\cite{cohen2022torchxrayvision}), with ImageNet-C-style corruptions as the per-input quality scalar ($\bar q = 1 - \text{severity}/5$). The model exhibits strong intrinsic quality-awareness ($\rho(H, \bar q) = -0.971$, $p < 10^{-150}$), and --- consistent with our hypothesis that TS reversal is specific to \emph{weakly} quality-aware backbones --- no reversal is observed (QCDI: $-0.026 \to -0.021$ under TS). QCTS gracefully converges to standard TS ($\alpha \approx 0.21$), confirming that the method does not degrade when the backbone is already quality-aware.

We also apply the same protocol to fundus retinopathy screening (APTOS 2019~\cite{aptos2019}, ViT-DR backbone publicly available on HuggingFace, $n{=}200$ test images, $n{=}400$ val), where the raw backbone exhibits weakly positive $\rho{=}+0.259$ ($p{<}10^{-4}$). Unlike the CheXpert setting, QCTS leaves both $\rho$ and QCDI unchanged ($\text{QCDI}_{\text{QCTS}}{=}+0.130$ vs.\ $\text{QCDI}_{\text{raw}}{=}+0.122$). The null result is expected and informative: the dermoscopy-trained 5-head IQA module is not calibrated to fundus degradation patterns, so the quality scalar that drives QCTS is misaligned with the domain-relevant calibration deficit. The contrast with the CheXpert result illustrates a scope condition for QCTS: it improves quality-aware calibration when the quality scalar captures the degradation dimensions that govern confidence in the target domain, and a domain-specific IQA is required otherwise.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%=====================================================================

The reason existing methods are quality-oblivious is structural. MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles inject uncertainty at the parameter level via stochastic forward passes and independent models, capturing epistemic but not input-dependent uncertainty: a blurry input leaves parameters unchanged, so dropout noise and ensemble variance follow the same distribution as for clean inputs. Input-dependent uncertainty therefore requires an explicit mechanism that conditions the predictive distribution on input properties. QCTS supplies this in the simplest possible form: temperature as a function of $\qbar$ adds a quality-dependent confidence penalty without modifying internal representations, separating \emph{what to predict} from \emph{how confident to be}. The same separation is also QCTS's ceiling: logit rescaling cannot repair quality-degraded feature extraction. On HAM10000 zero-shot Std VIB + QCTS reaches $\rho{=}-0.108$, an improvement over the uncalibrated $\rho{=}-0.033$ but still bounded above by what training-time conditioning---evidential losses~\cite{sensoy2018evidential}, quality-conditioned priors---could in principle achieve.

\textbf{Structural reason TS reverses on weakly quality-aware backbones.}
Standard TS chooses a single scalar $T$ to minimise validation NLL. Because $T$ is quality-independent it has zero degrees of freedom along $\qbar$, so the optimum is governed by whichever sub-population dominates the validation gradient. On a weakly quality-aware backbone the raw HQ--LQ entropy gap is small relative to logit magnitude, and the NLL-optimal $T$ contracts predictions toward mid-confidence roughly uniformly. Two regimes follow. If the entropy gap is small (Std VIB, raw $\rho{=}-0.153$), uniform contraction drags the LQ--HQ entropy ordering below sampling noise and the ITB-pool $\rho$ flips to $+0.241$. If the gap is wider (ViT-Tiny, raw $\rho{=}-0.160$ with sharper LQ over-confidence), the entropy $\rho$ survives but the NLL optimum trades HQ ECE for LQ ECE and QCDI flips ($+0.023\to-0.029$). On strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 (raw $\rho{=}-0.368$) the entropy--$\qbar$ signal is large enough to absorb a uniform rescale and TS is neutral. The conclusion: any quality-independent post-hoc correction has zero degrees of freedom along $\qbar$; quality-aware calibration needs at least one, and QCTS supplies exactly that via the slope $\alpha$ of $T(\qbar)$.

\textbf{Threshold sensitivity and alternative quality proxy.}
The full-pool $\rho$ sign-flip is not an artefact of the $\tau{=}0.45$ ITB-LQ threshold. Sweeping $\tau\in\{0.40,0.43,0.45,0.48,0.50\}$, within every LQ sub-pool TS makes $\rho$ \emph{more} negative (raw $\rho\in[-0.09,-0.01]$ vs.\ TS $\rho\in[-0.19,-0.05]$); reversal is concentrated in the HQ tier (raw $-0.140\to+0.217$), not LQ. A training-free Laplacian-variance proxy ($\rho{=}0.50$ with the 5-head IQA) yields an even stronger reversal (raw $-0.446\to+0.690$), so the phenomenon is not tethered to the learned estimator.

A manual audit of Std VIB confidently-wrong ITB-LQ predictions ($\hat p>0.85$) splits the errors into three modes: heavy blur with indiscernible lesion boundaries (49\%), colour-distorted blur with anomalous colour temperature (32\%), and diagnostically ambiguous lesions (19\%). The first two are exactly the dimensions QCTS softens most aggressively in Table~\ref{tab:perdeg}(a) (blur $-51\%$, colour $-41\%$), suggesting the same prototypes can guide targeted data augmentation in a future training-time fix.

\textbf{Limitations.}
Five caveats bound our claims. \emph{First, TS reversal is a property of weakly quality-aware backbones, not a universal architectural phenomenon}: on ResNet-50, TS is approximately neutral on both $\rho$ and QCDI. A wider architecture sweep (ConvNeXt, Swin, large ViTs) is left to future work. \emph{Second, ITB-LQ is built from programmatic degradations} applied to clinically-acquired images, so real-world artefacts (immersion-gel specular reflections, oblique phone-torch illumination, non-frontal framing) are not reproduced exactly. As a partial in-the-wild check, $174$ publicly available ISIC 2020 low-quality dermoscopy images give EfficientNet-B3 ECE $0.073$ $[0.038,0.125]$, below ITB-LQ ($0.146$); a cross-class evaluation with matched malignant low-quality images remains needed (supplementary). \emph{Third, QCTS is bounded above by the resolution of the IQA module}: it can only correct for quality signal the IQA stage already sees. \emph{Fourth, ITB uses a binary melanoma-vs-benign task}: 7--26-class dermoscopy settings have different ECE semantics. \emph{Fifth, whether reduced QCDI translates into better triage outcomes is a clinical question}; decision-curve analysis on ITB-LQ gives Std VIB + QCTS a higher maximum net benefit ($0.192$ vs.\ $0.186$ for Std VIB), and a $20\%$-budget triage simulation cuts the missed-positive rate from $73.3\%$ to $70.0\%$ (supplementary). A prospective study is needed to translate these proxies into clinical impact.

\textbf{Scope conditions for QCTS.}
The practical contract of QCTS is narrower than ``robustness to image quality'' and we flag three conditions readers should keep in mind. \emph{(i) Quality-signal availability and unbiasedness.} QCTS only helps when a per-input $\qbar$ is available, stable across the deployment pipeline, and not systematically biased by skin tone, lesion morphology, or device. If $\qbar$ is missing or systematically misestimated, the corrected logits collapse toward standard TS (when $\alpha\!\to\!0$) or, worse, misroute confidence the wrong way. ITB-LQ is defined by the same IQA scalar QCTS conditions on, so we partially break the tautology by reporting EfficientNet-B3 ECE on $174$ IQA-independent ``real-LQ'' images: ECE $0.073$ $[0.038,0.125]$, below ITB-LQ's $0.146$ (supp \S A18). \emph{(ii) Synthetic-to-real gap in the IQA stage.} The 5-head IQA used here is trained on \emph{programmatic} degradations (Gaussian blur, additive colour shifts, brightness, contrast, completeness crops). Real consumer-camera artefacts---specular reflections, motion blur with shake, banding, JPEG re-compression chains---are not in the training distribution, and the Fundus negative result (\S\ref{sec:generalisation}) is one concrete illustration of how an out-of-domain IQA collapses the QCTS gain. \emph{(iii) Calibration is not classification.} A lower QCDI or ECE does not imply safer triage: a perfectly calibrated model whose top class is wrong remains clinically unusable. QCTS rescales confidence around the model's existing decision boundary and does not relocate that boundary. The framework is therefore best read as a \emph{narrow calibration correction under a quality covariate}, not a general robustness fix.

\textbf{Estimator robustness.}
The headline numbers are robust to binning choice and tie-breaking. Across equal-width and equal-mass binning at $M\in\{10,15\}$, every method's full-pool ECE shifts by less than $0.025$ and the Std VIB + QCTS row remains lowest in every column (supp \S A17). A debiased ECE estimator~\cite{kumar2019verified} \emph{enlarges} the QCTS-vs-Std-VIB gap ($0.097\!\to\!0.027$ for QCTS vs.\ $0.097$ for Std VIB), ruling out per-bin sampling noise as a confound. Spearman $\rho$ under random tie-breaking on jittered entropies has standard deviation below $10^{-5}$ across 20 seeds, with $\geq 2{,}756/2{,}820$ unique entropy values per row; ties are not driving the reported sign-flip. Five-of-five $\bar q$-quintile ECE reductions survive Bonferroni correction at the family-wise $\alpha{=}0.005$ level.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%=====================================================================

We argued that medical-AI calibration should be evaluated as a function of input quality, not only in aggregate, and gave the idea three concrete handles: the QAC formulation, the QCDI scalar, and a three-class taxonomy. We instantiated these on ITB---a quality-stratified benchmark over four dermoscopy datasets---and proposed QCTS, a two-parameter post-hoc correction that cuts QCDI by $73\%$ over standard TS on Std VIB without retraining. The most consequential finding is that MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles, two of the most popular uncertainty methods, are quality-oblivious despite their reputation: their calibration collapses on the low-quality inputs that medical triage depends on. Natural next steps are to extend ITB beyond dermoscopy with modality-specific quality models, run a prospective study linking QCDI to triage outcomes, and derive quality-conditioned variants of richer post-hoc methods (e.g.\ conformal prediction).
