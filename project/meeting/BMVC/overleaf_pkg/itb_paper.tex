\title{Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling:\\
       Post-hoc Calibration under Image Quality Shift}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Problem.} Image quality varies across the inputs a deployed classifier receives, yet the post-hoc pipeline shipped in practice---a deterministic mean-latent forward followed by scalar temperature scaling (TS)---can silently reverse the entropy--quality correlation on a quality-fragile backbone, turning a model that was weakly more uncertain on low-quality inputs into one that is more confident on them. We trace the reversal to a forward-pass swap between training-time MC-marginalisation and deployment-time deterministic forward; scalar TS cannot undo this because it is monotonic in deterministic logits. On a strictly deterministic backbone the same TS objective instead flips a bin-based calibration discrepancy by trading high-quality for low-quality ECE; on a strongly quality-aware backbone the pipeline is neutral.
\textbf{Method.} We propose Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling (QCTS), a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator that adds exactly one slope along a per-input quality scalar $\bar q$---the first post-hoc calibrator with the degree of freedom required to undo the reversal. Alongside QCTS we define Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC), the Quality-Calibration Degradation Index (QCDI), and a quality-oblivious/fragile/aware taxonomy, instantiated on the Image Triage Benchmark (ITB) over ISIC~2020, FitzPatrick17k, HAM10000, and PAD-UFES.
\textbf{Result.} QCTS substantially reduces both QCDI and low-quality ECE on a Std~VIB backbone, transfers across five backbone families and to ImageNet-C corruption severity as an a-priori quality scalar, and preserves the directional gain cross-dataset on HAM10000 and PAD-UFES; MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles remain quality-oblivious, with low-quality ECE several times higher than Std~VIB. We will release ITB and QCTS.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%=====================================================================

A deployed medical classifier sees images of widely varying quality, yet calibration is still reported as a single aggregate number~\cite{codella2018skin,guo2017calibration}. The subset that matters most for triage---blurred, poorly lit, or off-frame inputs---is precisely where confident-but-wrong predictions cause downstream harm, and precisely where standard reporting is silent~\cite{esteva2017dermatologist,tschandl2020human}.

Existing calibrators target the i.i.d.\ setting~\cite{guo2017calibration,ovadia2019uncertainty,gal2016dropout,lakshminarayanan2017ensemble}. None conditions on per-input quality. We make this dependence explicit through a per-image quality scalar $\bar q$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}) and ask how each calibrator behaves as a function of $\bar q$.

\begin{figure*}[!tbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig_method}
  \caption{\textbf{Method overview.}
    \textbf{(a)} \emph{ITB construction}: four dermoscopy datasets are programmatically degraded across five quality dimensions, scored by a 5-head IQA module to produce a per-image $\bar q \in [0,1]$, and partitioned into the LQ/Edge/HQ/Diverse subsets used for evaluation.
    \textbf{(b)} \emph{QCTS}: on a frozen backbone, the logits $z$ are rescaled by a quality-conditioned temperature $T(\bar q) = \mathrm{softplus}(T_0 + \alpha(1{-}\bar q))$; the two parameters $T_0, \alpha$ are fit by L-BFGS on the validation set, and $\alpha\!=\!0$ recovers standard temperature scaling.
    \emph{Takeaway:} ITB and QCTS share a single per-image $\bar q$, so adapting the framework to a new modality reduces to swapping the IQA module.}
  \label{fig:method}
\end{figure*}

A counter-intuitive observation motivates our method. The pipeline shipped in practice---a deterministic mean-latent forward followed by scalar temperature scaling (TS)---does not just fail to make a quality-fragile backbone aware of quality; it \emph{reverses} the sign of the entropy--quality correlation, turning weak quality-awareness into active quality-confusion (Table~\ref{tab:main}). The reversal is a property of the deployable pipeline, not of TS as a calibrator: on a deterministic backbone TS preserves rank but flips the binned discrepancy instead, and on a strongly quality-aware backbone the pipeline is neutral.

We address this with three contributions. \textbf{(i)} A protocol---Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC), the Quality-Calibration Degradation Index (QCDI), and an oblivious/fragile/aware taxonomy---that surfaces the failure aggregate ECE hides. \textbf{(ii)} The Image Triage Benchmark (ITB), four dermoscopy datasets stratified by $\bar q$, on which we evaluate seven calibration paradigms and five backbone families. \textbf{(iii)} Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling (QCTS), a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator that adds one slope along $\bar q$---the minimal degree of freedom needed to undo the reversal---and cuts QCDI by $73\%$ over TS on Std~VIB without retraining.

\section{Related Work}
%=====================================================================

\textbf{Calibration.}
Modern networks are routinely miscalibrated~\cite{guo2017calibration}, and temperature scaling~\cite{guo2017calibration} remains the standard post-hoc fix. Bayesian approximations---MC Dropout~\cite{gal2016dropout}, Deep Ensembles~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble,ovadia2019uncertainty}---inject parameter-level uncertainty but not input-dependent uncertainty~\cite{malinin2019predictive}; Evidential Deep Learning~\cite{sensoy2018evidential} adds input-dependent uncertainty through a Dirichlet output at training time. Throughout, ``Std~VIB'' is an EfficientNet-B3 trained with the Variational Information Bottleneck~\cite{alemi2017vib} at prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

\emph{Where QCTS sits.} The closest existing post-hoc family is conditional calibration. Multicalibration~\cite{hebert2018multicalibration,deng2023happymap} and local calibration~\cite{luo2023local} require pre-specified groups or kernels; multi-domain TS~\cite{yu2022robust} assumes a known discrete partition; quality-binned isotonic regression~\cite{zadrozny2002transforming} drives $\rho(H,\qbar)$ positive in our setting (quality-confused, \S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis}). All three discretise the conditioning signal. QCTS is the first post-hoc calibrator that conditions on a \emph{continuous} per-input scalar and adds exactly one degree of freedom along it---the minimum required, by Proposition~\ref{prop:ts_rank}, to escape the rank inversion that scalar TS provably cannot undo.

\textbf{Medical-AI benchmarks and IQA.}
Curated benchmarks~\cite{irvin2019chexpert,johnson2019mimic,menze2014multimodal,codella2018skin} prioritise discriminative accuracy on clean data; distribution-shift work~\cite{koh2021wilds,subbaswamy2021evaluating,seyyed2021underdiagnosis} examines dataset and subgroup shift, but none systematically evaluates how \emph{image quality} affects \emph{calibration quality}. Dermoscopic blind IQA~\cite{barata2023quality,park2023quality} produces a single quality score; we instead use a 5-head IQA module trained on synthetic degradations (supp \S A1). Teledermatology deployment studies~\cite{esteva2017dermatologist,tschandl2020human,winkler2022dermoscopy,liu2020deep} highlight degradation on non-clinical images; ITB+QCTS supply the missing evaluation protocol and lightweight remedy.

\section{Quality-Aware Calibration}
\label{sec:qac}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{Preliminaries and QAC/QCDI}

For a classifier $f_\theta:\mathcal{X}\to\Delta^{K-1}$, Expected Calibration Error~\cite{guo2017calibration} bins predictions into $M$ equal-width confidence bins $B_m$ and returns $\mathrm{ECE}=\sum_m\frac{|B_m|}{N}|\mathrm{acc}(B_m){-}\mathrm{conf}(B_m)|$. ECE is aggregated over the whole pool; under systematic quality variation it can be moderate while calibration collapses on a critical stratum. Let $q(x)\in[0,1]^5$ be a 5-head IQA quality vector (sharpness, brightness, completeness, colour temperature, contrast; supp \S A1), $\qbar(x){=}\tfrac{1}{5}\sum_i q_i(x)$, and partition $[0,1]$ into strata $\Q_\ell{=}\{x:\qbar(x)\in[\tau_{\ell-1},\tau_\ell)\}$. We define \textbf{Quality-Aware Calibration} $\mathrm{QAC}_\ell$ as ECE restricted to $\Q_\ell$, and the \textbf{Quality-Calibration Degradation Index}
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{QCDI} = \mathrm{QAC}_\text{low} - \mathrm{QAC}_\text{high},\quad \qbar_\text{low}{<}0.45,\ \qbar_\text{high}{>}0.50.
\label{eq:qcdi}
\end{equation}
The intermediate band $[0.45,0.50]$ is reserved for the Edge stratum and omitted from QCDI. Positive QCDI means calibration degrades on low-quality inputs; near zero indicates quality-invariant calibration. We also track predictive entropy $H(p){=}{-}\sum_k p_k\log p_k$ and its Spearman correlation $\rho(H,\qbar)$: a quality-aware model has $\rho{<}0$ (more uncertainty on lower-quality inputs).

\subsection{A Taxonomy of Calibration under Quality Shift}
\label{sec:taxonomy}

Three behavioural regimes by QCDI magnitude: \textbf{Quality-Oblivious} ($\mathrm{QCDI}{>}0.10$; LQ ECE $\gg$ HQ ECE; representative: MC Dropout, Deep Ensembles), \textbf{Quality-Fragile} ($0{<}\mathrm{QCDI}{\le}0.10$; mild quality sensitivity; Std~VIB, Focal+LS), and \textbf{Quality-Aware} ($\mathrm{QCDI}{\le}0$ with $\rho{<}-0.15$, see below; our QCTS). Quality-oblivious methods require structural change; quality-fragile methods are fixable with lightweight post-hoc correction.

\textbf{Disambiguating QCDI sign-flips.}
Negative QCDI alone is insufficient. A method is \emph{genuinely quality-aware} only when it is paired with negative $\rho$; it is \emph{quality-confused} when QCDI${\le}0$ comes from inflating HQ ECE rather than reducing LQ ECE, with $\rho$ flat or positive. ViT-Tiny$+$TS illustrates the latter and ViT-Tiny$+$QCTS the former (Table~\ref{tab:universality}); thresholds and the permutation noise floor are in supp \S A14.

\begin{proposition}[Scalar TS preserves entropy rank, binary case]
\label{prop:ts_rank}
Let $d(x){=}z_1(x){-}z_0(x)$ be the binary logit margin and $H_T(x){=}h\!\left(\sigma(d(x)/T)\right)$ with $h$ the binary entropy and $\sigma$ the logistic. For any $T{>}0$ uniform across inputs,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{rank}\{H_T(x_i)\}_{i=1}^{N} \;=\; \mathrm{rank}\{H_1(x_i)\}_{i=1}^{N},
\label{eq:rank_invariance}
\end{equation}
so the Spearman correlation $\rho(H_T,\bar q)$ is invariant to scalar TS.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$h$ is symmetric and strictly concave on $[0,1]$ with max at $\tfrac12$, and $\sigma$ is strictly monotone, hence $h(\sigma(u))$ is strictly decreasing in $|u|$. Since $T{>}0$ is shared across $x_i$, $|d(x_i)/T|$ has the same ordering as $|d(x_i)|$, giving \eqref{eq:rank_invariance}; Spearman depends only on ranks. $\square$
\end{proof}
Proposition~\ref{prop:ts_rank} is the formal statement of why scalar TS cannot undo a $\rho$ reversal once it is introduced upstream---used in the forward-ablation decomposition of \S\ref{sec:main_results} and the discussion of \S\ref{sec:discussion}.

\section{ITB Benchmark and QCTS Method}
\label{sec:method}
%=====================================================================

We describe ITB (\S\ref{sec:itb}, Fig.~\ref{fig:method}a) and QCTS (\S\ref{sec:qcts}, Fig.~\ref{fig:method}b).

\subsection{ITB: Image Triage Benchmark}
\label{sec:itb}

ITB partitions a held-out test pool by $\qbar$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}a) into ITB-LQ ($\qbar{<}0.45$, $n{=}300$, the clinically critical subset), ITB-Edge ($\qbar\in[0.45,0.50]$, $n{=}660$, the band reserved between LQ and HQ in the QCDI definition), ITB-HQ ($\qbar{>}0.50$, $n{=}360$), and ITB-Diverse ($n{=}1{,}500$ FitzPatrick17k images covering skin types I--VI). The contribution is the quality-stratified evaluation \emph{methodology} and the QCDI metric; any labelled image set with an associated quality scalar adopts the same protocol. \textbf{Baselines (7).} EfficientNet-B3 (deterministic); Std~VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib} with prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$; Focal Loss + Label Smoothing; MC Dropout (30 passes, rate~0.2)~\cite{gal2016dropout}; Deep Ensemble (5$\times$ Std~VIB)~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble}; post-hoc TS~\cite{guo2017calibration} on Std~VIB; Evidential Deep Learning~\cite{sensoy2018evidential}. Architecture probes (ResNet-50, ViT-Tiny, ConvNeXt-Tiny, Swin-Tiny) appear in \S\ref{sec:universality}. \textbf{Datasets.} ISIC~2020 ($33{,}126$, 70/10/20) is the primary corpus; HAM10000~\cite{tschandl2019ham10000} and PAD-UFES~\cite{pacheco2020pad} provide zero-shot transfer pools; programmatic degradation and 5-head IQA scoring yield $149{,}100$ images with 5-dimensional quality annotations.

\subsection{Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling}
\label{sec:qcts}

Standard TS learns a single $T\in\R^+$ minimising validation NLL; because $T$ is uniform across inputs, it cannot differentially adjust confidence for degraded images. QCTS replaces $T$ with a continuous function of $\qbar(x)$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}b):
\begin{equation}
T(\qbar) = \mathrm{softplus}\big(T_0 + \alpha (1 - \qbar)\big),\qquad T_0\in\R,\ \alpha\in\R_{\ge 0},
\label{eq:qcts}
\end{equation}
with $\mathrm{softplus}(u){=}\log(1{+}e^u)$. Eq.~\ref{eq:qcts} is a \emph{design choice}: we list three inductive biases any quality-conditioned temperature should respect---\emph{(B1) Monotonicity} ($\qbar\mapsto T$ non-increasing, so degraded inputs are softened more), \emph{(B2) Positivity} ($T{>}0$), \emph{(B3) Smoothness and TS reduction} ($T$ smooth in $\qbar$ and recovers TS at $\alpha{=}0$). Eq.~\ref{eq:qcts} satisfies all three. Let $u(\qbar){=}T_0{+}\alpha(1{-}\qbar)$. Differentiating and using $\mathrm{softplus}'{=}\sigma$ (logistic):
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial T}{\partial \qbar} \;=\; \sigma\!\big(u(\qbar)\big)\cdot(-\alpha) \;\le\; 0\quad\text{for } \alpha{\ge}0,
\label{eq:b1}
\end{equation}
giving B1. B2 holds because $\mathrm{softplus}(u){>}0$ for all $u\in\R$, so the search over $T_0,\alpha$ is unconstrained. B3 holds because $T$ is $C^\infty$ in $\qbar$ and at $\alpha{=}0$ collapses to $T{\equiv}\mathrm{softplus}(T_0)$, a constant temperature; reparametrising $T_0{=}\mathrm{softplus}^{-1}(T_\mathrm{TS})$ recovers standard TS exactly. These three are necessary but not sufficient---infinitely many functions satisfy them. We then ablate among the families that minimally add a quality slope: linear $T_0{+}\alpha(1{-}\qbar)$ violates B2 when $T_0{+}\alpha{<}0$; ReLU breaks B3 at the rectification point; piecewise-constant minimises validation NLL but produces the worst QCDI (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}). Softplus is the smallest-parameter monotone-positive-smooth form we tested; we report results on it and treat it as the simplest defensible default rather than a derived optimum. With $f_\theta$ frozen we fit $(T_0,\alpha)$ by L-BFGS on the same NLL objective as TS, $-\sum_{(x,y)\in\D_\text{val}}\log\mathrm{softmax}(z(x)/T(\qbar(x)))_y$, initialised at zero, up to 200 iterations.

\textbf{Connection to Information Bottleneck.}
Larger $T$ flattens the predictive distribution analogously to a higher VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib} compression $\beta$; QCTS makes this compression quality-dependent ($T(\qbar{=}0){\approx}2.24$ vs.\ $T(\qbar{=}1){\approx}1.44$ on Std~VIB, $56\%$ higher on LQ). A PAC-Bayes motivation~\cite{mcallester1999pac} is in supp \S A22.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}
%=====================================================================

Our experiments fix the training and evaluation protocol (\S\ref{sec:setup}), rank seven baselines on ITB (\S\ref{sec:main_results}), isolate QCTS and inspect the learned $T(\qbar)$ (\S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis}), probe universality across backbones (\S\ref{sec:universality}), and verify zero-shot, fairness, and quality-scalar transfer (\S\ref{sec:generalisation}).

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

All models trained on ISIC~2020 (70/10/20). EfficientNet-based models use AdamW with cosine annealing ($\text{lr}{=}10^{-4}$, batch~128, 90~epochs); MC Dropout uses 30 passes at dropout~0.2; Deep Ensemble combines 5 Std~VIB; TS/QCTS L-BFGS for 200 iters. All training fits on a single RTX~4070~8GB. Metrics: AUC; ECE ($M{=}15$); QCDI; Spearman $\rho(H,\qbar)$. Bootstrap with $n{=}10^4$ resamples; metrics averaged over 3 seeds.

\subsection{Main Results and Calibration Taxonomy}
\label{sec:main_results}

\textbf{Aggregate performance hides the failure mode.}
Table~\ref{tab:main} ranks seven calibration paradigms. The two methods with the highest ITB-LQ AUC---MC Dropout and Deep Ensemble---also carry the worst LQ ECE, both roughly three times Std~VIB's, and both land in the Quality-Oblivious band (QCDI${>}0.10$). Restricted to ITB-LQ alone, MC Dropout's $\rho$ flips positive ($+0.350$) and Deep Ensemble's becomes mildly positive: a Simpson-style divergence where aggregate quality-awareness disappears precisely on the stratum that motivates the metric. TS leaves QCDI essentially unchanged, and EDL---the one training-time quality-aware baseline---sits at Quality-Fragile. Only Std~VIB$+$QCTS is genuinely quality-aware on both criteria: the strongest negative $\rho$ in the table and QCDI an order of magnitude smaller than uncalibrated Std~VIB. Reliability diagrams in supp \S A18 make this visible: MC Dropout falls well off the diagonal on ITB-LQ while QCTS tracks it closely.

\textbf{TS reverses the entropy--quality correlation on Std~VIB.}
Raw Std~VIB has $\rho{=}-0.153$; after the deployable pipeline, $\rho{=}+0.241$ (Table~\ref{tab:main}). Write the predictive distribution along the pipeline as
\begin{equation}
\underbrace{\E_{z\sim\mathcal{N}(\mu,\Sigma)}\!\big[\sigma(z)\big]}_{p_a(x):\;\text{MC, raw}}
\;\;\to\;\;
\underbrace{\sigma\!\big(\mu(x)\big)}_{p_b(x):\;\text{det.-}\mu,\;T{=}1}
\;\;\to\;\;
\underbrace{\sigma\!\big(\mu(x)/T_\mathrm{TS}\big)}_{p_c(x):\;\text{det.-}\mu,\;T{=}T_\mathrm{TS}},
\label{eq:pipeline}
\end{equation}
and let $\rho_\bullet{=}\rho(H(p_\bullet),\bar q)$. Empirically $\rho_a{=}{-}0.163$, $\rho_b{=}{+}0.241$, $\rho_c{=}{+}0.241$, so $\Delta\rho_{a\to b}{=}{+}0.404$ ($100\%$ of the flip) and $\Delta\rho_{b\to c}{<}10^{-5}$. The $b{\to}c$ vanishing follows directly from Proposition~\ref{prop:ts_rank}: $p_b$ and $p_c$ share the deterministic margin $\mu(x)$ up to a global $1/T_\mathrm{TS}$ rescale, so ranks (and hence Spearman) are identical. The $a{\to}b$ flip is the substantive one: collapsing the MC marginal $\E_z[\sigma(z)]$ to the deterministic mean $\sigma(\mu)$ discards the input-dependent variance $\Sigma(x)$ that carried the negative quality correlation. TS as an objective is innocent of the rank flip; the deployable pipeline introduces it upstream, and scalar TS provably cannot undo it.

On ViT-Tiny (strictly deterministic, no MC component) the rank-confound vanishes and TS instead flips the binned discrepancy---ECE-HQ inflates and QCDI crosses zero---the within-forward consequence of the TS objective on a bin-based metric. On the strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 the pipeline is neutral on both metrics. QCTS keeps QCDI near zero and strengthens negative $\rho$ on every backbone tested (Table~\ref{tab:universality}).

\subsection{QCTS Analysis}
\label{sec:qcts_analysis}

On a frozen Std~VIB, QCTS roughly halves ECE on ITB-LQ while leaving ITB-HQ ECE essentially unchanged (Table~\ref{tab:main}): it softens low-quality predictions without paying for it on high-quality ones, and QCDI drops by $73\%$ over TS into the Quality-Aware regime. Across three seeds the fitted slope $\alpha\in\{0.34, 0.37, 0.96\}$ all land in this regime (QCDI${\le}0.006$); the NLL landscape is a shallow basin in $\alpha$ rather than multi-modal (supp \S A17), and we adopt the median-seed default $\alpha{=}0.37$.

\textbf{Functional form and where QCTS fails.}
Table~\ref{tab:ablation} compares softplus against linear and 3-bin piecewise-constant temperatures: piecewise wins validation NLL but loses on QCDI and on entropy--$\bar q$ correlation, showing NLL is not a sufficient training signal. Per-degradation, QCTS cuts ITB-LQ ECE on blur ($51\%$) and colour-temperature ($41\%$), is mildly helpful on brightness, and---honestly---harms contrast-degraded inputs ($+58\%$ ECE) because scalar temperature shrinks logit magnitudes but cannot restore the relative geometry a contrast attack flattens. This is the ceiling of any post-hoc logit calibrator. Per quality quintile, every quintile improves, with the largest gain on the lowest 20\% (supp \S A12).

\input{table2_ablation}

Coarser conditioning fails in a characteristic way. Quality-stratified Platt scaling and quality-binned isotonic regression both reach QCDI${<}0$ but invert $\rho$, compressing LQ predictions into narrow confidence bands---the quality-confused failure of \S\ref{sec:taxonomy}. Substituting BRISQUE, CLIP-IQA, or Laplacian variance for the 5-head IQA collapses QCTS to TS or pushes QCDI positive (supp \S A2/A10). QCTS is the only configuration that reaches QCDI${\approx}0$ and the most negative $\rho$ simultaneously.

\subsection{Universality across Backbone Families}
\label{sec:universality}

We re-fit QCTS on four additional backbones---ResNet-50, ViT-Tiny (DeiT init.), ConvNeXt-Tiny, Swin-Tiny---trained on ISIC~2020 with the same protocol; Table~\ref{tab:universality} reports three in the main body, the rest in supp \S A13.

Reversal manifests differently on each backbone (Table~\ref{tab:universality}): a $\rho$ sign flip on Std~VIB, a QCDI flip via HQ inflation on ViT-Tiny and Swin-Tiny, and approximate neutrality on the strongly quality-aware ResNet-50. Reversal is therefore a property of \emph{weakly} quality-aware backbones, not of TS as a calibrator; the structural reason is in \S\ref{sec:discussion}.

QCTS is consistent across all five: $\rho$ becomes more negative on every backbone, while QCDI gains concentrate where there is room to gain---Std~VIB, ViT-Tiny, and ConvNeXt move clearly toward zero, ResNet-50 (already at $+0.014$) is unchanged. The fitted slope $\alpha$ correlates inversely with raw quality-awareness, from ViT-Tiny's $\alpha{=}1.40$ to ResNet-50's $\alpha{=}0.24$. On a 20-bin sweep of $\bar q$, the fitted softplus tracks the per-bin optimum $T^*(\bar q)$ on ViT-Tiny but is nearly flat on ResNet-50 (supp \S A23): QCTS reduces to TS exactly when reduction is appropriate.

\subsection{Generalisation, Fairness, and Cross-Scalar Transfer}
\label{sec:generalisation}

Four external checks confirm QCTS is not an ISIC artefact (per-row numbers in Table~\ref{tab:transfer}).

\textbf{Cross-dataset.}
On HAM10000 and PAD-UFES, QCTS preserves the negative entropy--$\bar q$ correlation; the TS reversal resurfaces on both unseen pools, reproducing the failure mode on independent dermoscopy and smartphone-clinical data.

\textbf{Fairness.}
On the FitzPatrick17k V--VI cohort, QCTS pushes $\rho$ more negative, directionally consistent with the gain on lighter skin types but estimated on small $n$; we omit per-skin QCDI for type VI as unreliable (supp \S A5).

\textbf{Quality-scalar transfer to ImageNet-C.}
Substituting corruption severity for the learned $\bar q$ on the 300 LQ images, QCTS strengthens $\rho$ on \emph{all 18} corruptions for both ResNet-50 and ViT-Tiny, while TS is inert ($|\Delta\rho|{<}10^{-4}$). Severity needs no learned IQA to act as a quality scalar.

\textbf{Cross-modality scope.}
On chest X-ray (DenseNet-121, intrinsically quality-aware), QCTS reduces gracefully to TS ($\alpha{\approx}0.21$); on fundus (APTOS 2019), the dermoscopy-trained IQA is mis-aligned and QCTS is inert---a scope condition on the IQA module, not a contradiction of the method.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%=====================================================================

MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles inject uncertainty at the parameter level---a blurry input leaves parameters unchanged, so dropout noise and ensemble variance follow the same distribution as for clean inputs. Input-dependent uncertainty therefore requires an explicit mechanism that conditions the predictive distribution on input properties. QCTS supplies this in the simplest form: temperature as a function of $\bar q$ adds a quality-dependent confidence penalty without touching internal representations, separating \emph{what to predict} from \emph{how confident to be}. The same separation is also QCTS's ceiling: logit rescaling cannot repair quality-degraded feature extraction.

The deployable pipeline (deterministic-$\mu$ forward $+$ scalar $T$) has zero degrees of freedom along $\bar q$ and inherits whatever quality dependence the deterministic forward exposes: a $\rho$ flip on Std~VIB (Eq.~\ref{eq:pipeline}), a binned QCDI flip on ViT-Tiny, neutrality on ResNet-50. The fix is the same in all three cases---add at least one degree of freedom along $\bar q$, which QCTS does through the slope $\alpha$. A $\tau$ sweep and a confidently-wrong failure-mode audit (blur 49\% / colour 32\% / ambiguous 19\%, matching the dimensions QCTS softens most) appear in supp \S A8/A9.

\textbf{Anticipated objections and our position.}
We list the strongest attacks against this work and our honest response, rather than burying them in supplementary.

\emph{(A1) ``ITB is synthetic degradation, not in-the-wild.''}
True. As partial validation we ran EfficientNet-B3 on 174 ISIC real low-quality dermoscopy images and recovered ECE $0.073$ ($95\%$ CI $[0.038, 0.125]$), below ITB-LQ's $0.146$ in the same direction. A matched-malignant in-the-wild cohort is the next step (supp \S A7); we do not claim deployment evidence.

\emph{(A2) ``Clinical impact is not established.''}
Correct. Decision-curve analysis on ITB-LQ gives QCTS max net benefit $0.192$ ($95\%$ CI $[0.148, 0.242$]) vs.\ Std~VIB $0.186$ ($[0.145, 0.233]$, supp \S A3): the CIs overlap and the absolute gap is not significant at $n{=}300$. We explicitly do not pitch QCTS as a triage system; a prospective study is required.

\emph{(A3) ``Incremental over multi-domain TS / quality-binned isotonic.''}
The discrete-conditioning alternatives we benchmark do reach QCDI${<}0$ but \emph{invert} $\rho$ (\S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis}, supp \S A10), entering the quality-confused regime. Proposition~\ref{prop:ts_rank} explains why: scalar TS within any bin cannot change rank, so the only way to add quality-awareness is a continuous slope across $\bar q$. QCTS is the minimal such fix.

\emph{(A4) ``Reversal is an artefact of one weak backbone (Std VIB, AUC $0.553$).''}
Reversal manifests differently on three of five backbones (Std VIB $\rho$-flip; ViT-Tiny + Swin-Tiny QCDI-flip; ResNet-50 neutral). The backbone-dependence is the point, not a flaw---the paper's framing is explicit that reversal is a property of \emph{weakly} quality-aware backbones, not an architectural law.

\emph{(A5) ``Why softplus and not learned?''}
We tested linear, ReLU, and piecewise-constant (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}); piecewise minimises val-NLL but loses on QCDI and on $\rho$, showing NLL is not a sufficient training signal. Softplus is the smallest-parameter form satisfying B1--B3 (\S\ref{sec:qcts}, Eq.~\ref{eq:b1}) and beats the alternatives on the metrics that matter.

\emph{(A6) ``ECE estimator is brittle.''}
Equal-width vs.\ equal-mass binning at $M\in\{10,15\}$ and debiased ECE~\cite{kumar2019verified} all preserve direction; debiased ECE \emph{enlarges} the QCTS gap (supp \S A20).

\textbf{Out-of-scope.}
QCTS is bounded above by the IQA module's resolution---the Fundus null result (\S\ref{sec:generalisation}) shows what happens when an out-of-domain IQA fails. Multi-class semantics differ from the binary ECE used here and are not evaluated.

%=====================================================================
\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%=====================================================================

Medical-AI calibration should be evaluated as a function of input quality, not only in aggregate. We gave the idea three handles---QAC, QCDI, a quality-oblivious/fragile/aware taxonomy---instantiated them on ITB, and proposed QCTS, a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator that cuts QCDI by $73\%$ over standard TS on Std~VIB without retraining and transfers across five backbone families. The consequential finding is that MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles---two popular uncertainty methods---are quality-oblivious on the low-quality inputs that medical triage depends on. Natural next steps: modality-specific IQA modules, a prospective study linking QCDI to triage outcomes, and quality-conditioned variants of richer post-hoc methods.
