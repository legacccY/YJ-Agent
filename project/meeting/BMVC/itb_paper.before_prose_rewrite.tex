\title{Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling:\\
       Post-hoc Calibration under Image Quality Shift}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Problem.} Image quality varies across the inputs a deployed classifier receives, yet the post-hoc pipeline shipped in practice---a deterministic mean-latent forward followed by scalar temperature scaling (TS)---can silently reverse the entropy--quality correlation on a quality-fragile backbone, turning a model that was weakly more uncertain on low-quality inputs into one that is more confident on them. We trace the reversal to a forward-pass swap between training-time MC-marginalisation and deployment-time deterministic forward; scalar TS cannot undo this because it is monotonic in deterministic logits. On a strictly deterministic backbone the same TS objective instead flips a bin-based calibration discrepancy by trading high-quality for low-quality ECE; on a strongly quality-aware backbone the pipeline is neutral.
\textbf{Method.} We propose Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling (QCTS), a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator that adds exactly one slope along a per-input quality scalar $\bar q$---the first post-hoc calibrator with the degree of freedom required to undo the reversal. Alongside QCTS we define Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC), the Quality-Calibration Degradation Index (QCDI), and a quality-oblivious/fragile/aware taxonomy, instantiated on the Image Triage Benchmark (ITB) over ISIC~2020, FitzPatrick17k, HAM10000, and PAD-UFES.
\textbf{Result.} QCTS substantially reduces both QCDI and low-quality ECE on a Std~VIB backbone, transfers across five backbone families and to ImageNet-C corruption severity as an a-priori quality scalar, and preserves the directional gain cross-dataset on HAM10000 and PAD-UFES; MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles remain quality-oblivious, with low-quality ECE several times higher than Std~VIB. We will release ITB and QCTS.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%=====================================================================

Medical-AI calibration is typically evaluated as an aggregate quantity (global ECE, NLL) on curated clinical datasets~\cite{codella2018skin,tschandl2019ham10000,guo2017calibration}, yet real evaluation pools contain images of widely varying quality~\cite{esteva2017dermatologist,tschandl2020human}. Whether a calibrator remains reliable on the low-quality subset---blur, poor lighting, colour distortion, framing errors---is not part of the standard reporting protocol, even though that subset is where confident-but-wrong predictions matter most for downstream triage.

Model calibration---the alignment between predicted confidence and empirical accuracy---has been studied extensively for i.i.d.\ settings~\cite{guo2017calibration,ovadia2019uncertainty}. Methods from temperature scaling~\cite{guo2017calibration} to Monte Carlo Dropout~\cite{gal2016dropout} and deep ensembles~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble} are designed under the assumption that training and deployment distributions match. Real-world deployment violates this: image quality varies dramatically across users, devices, and environments, and calibration can collapse on the very inputs that most demand caution. Our framework (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}) partitions a labelled pool by a per-image quality scalar $\bar q$ and conditions the post-hoc temperature on $\bar q$.

\begin{figure*}[!tbp]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\linewidth]{figures/fig_method}
  \caption{\textbf{Method overview.}
    \textbf{(a)} \emph{ITB construction}: four dermoscopy datasets are programmatically degraded across five quality dimensions, scored by a 5-head IQA module to produce a per-image $\bar q \in [0,1]$, and partitioned into the LQ/Edge/HQ/Diverse subsets used for evaluation.
    \textbf{(b)} \emph{QCTS}: on a frozen backbone, the logits $z$ are rescaled by a quality-conditioned temperature $T(\bar q) = \mathrm{softplus}(T_0 + \alpha(1{-}\bar q))$; the two parameters $T_0, \alpha$ are fit by L-BFGS on the validation set, and $\alpha\!=\!0$ recovers standard temperature scaling.
    \emph{Takeaway:} ITB and QCTS share a single per-image $\bar q$, so adapting the framework to a new modality reduces to swapping the IQA module.}
  \label{fig:method}
\end{figure*}

We argue that this failure mode---which we term \textbf{quality-oblivious calibration}---is a blind spot in contemporary medical-AI evaluation. Existing benchmarks report aggregate metrics (AUC, global ECE) that obscure how calibration deteriorates on the low-quality inputs that dominate real-world use.

A counter-intuitive observation motivates our method. The post-hoc pipeline shipped in practice---a deterministic-$\mu$ forward followed by scalar temperature scaling~\cite{guo2017calibration}---does not merely fail to make a quality-fragile backbone (Std~VIB, $n{=}2{,}820$) quality-aware; it \emph{reverses} the sign of the entropy--quality Spearman correlation, taking the MC-marginalised raw $\rho{=}-0.163$ to deployment-time $\rho{=}+0.241$ (Table~\ref{tab:main}). A 3-way forward ablation (\S\ref{sec:main_results}) attributes the flip to the MC$\to$deterministic forward swap ($\Delta\rho{=}+0.404$, $100\%$ of the effect); scalar TS itself preserves the rank ($\Delta\rho{<}10^{-5}$, consistent with the fact that scalar TS is monotonic in deterministic logits). \S\ref{sec:universality} shows that on ViT-Tiny---a strictly deterministic classifier with no MC component---the within-forward effect of TS shows up instead as a QCDI sign-flip (raw $+0.023\to$ TS $-0.029$), while a strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 absorbs the pipeline without inversion.

In response, we propose Quality-Aware Calibration (QAC) as a dimension of evaluation (\S\ref{sec:qac}): a scalar QCDI and a quality-oblivious/fragile/aware taxonomy. We instantiate it as the Image Triage Benchmark (ITB, \S\ref{sec:itb}) over four dermoscopy datasets and evaluate seven methods spanning discriminative, Bayesian, ensemble, evidential, and post-hoc paradigms, with architecture probes on four additional backbones (\S\ref{sec:universality}). As a deployable remedy we propose Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling (QCTS, \S\ref{sec:qcts}): a two-parameter post-hoc correction that conditions the TS temperature on the per-image quality score, cutting QCDI from $+0.015$ to $+0.004$ on Std~VIB ($73\%$ over TS) without retraining.

\section{Related Work}
%=====================================================================

\textbf{Calibration.}
Modern networks are routinely miscalibrated~\cite{guo2017calibration}. Temperature scaling~\cite{guo2017calibration} remains the standard post-hoc calibrator. Bayesian approximations---MC Dropout~\cite{gal2016dropout}, Deep Ensembles~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble,ovadia2019uncertainty}---estimate epistemic but not input-dependent uncertainty~\cite{malinin2019predictive}; Evidential Deep Learning~\cite{sensoy2018evidential} models input-dependent uncertainty at training time via a Dirichlet output. The Variational Information Bottleneck~\cite{alemi2017vib} regularises a stochastic latent $Z$; throughout, ``Std~VIB'' is an EfficientNet-B3 trained with the VIB objective at prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$. Conditional and group-calibration methods (multicalibration~\cite{hebert2018multicalibration,deng2023happymap}, local calibration~\cite{luo2023local}) require pre-specified groups or kernels, not a continuous quality signal. Multi-domain TS~\cite{yu2022robust} assumes a known partition; quality-binned isotonic regression~\cite{zadrozny2002transforming} drives $\rho(H,\qbar)$ positive (quality-confused, \S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis}). QCTS fills the gap.

\textbf{Medical-AI benchmarks and IQA.}
Curated benchmarks~\cite{irvin2019chexpert,johnson2019mimic,menze2014multimodal,codella2018skin} prioritise discriminative accuracy on clean data; distribution-shift work~\cite{koh2021wilds,subbaswamy2021evaluating,seyyed2021underdiagnosis} examines dataset and subgroup shift, but none systematically evaluates how \emph{image quality} affects \emph{calibration quality}. Dermoscopic blind IQA~\cite{barata2023quality,park2023quality} produces a single quality score; we instead use a 5-head IQA module trained on synthetic degradations (supp \S A1). Teledermatology deployment studies~\cite{esteva2017dermatologist,tschandl2020human,winkler2022dermoscopy,liu2020deep} highlight degradation on non-clinical images; ITB+QCTS supply the missing evaluation protocol and lightweight remedy.

\section{Quality-Aware Calibration}
\label{sec:qac}
%=====================================================================

\subsection{Preliminaries and QAC/QCDI}

For a classifier $f_\theta:\mathcal{X}\to\Delta^{K-1}$, Expected Calibration Error~\cite{guo2017calibration} bins predictions into $M$ equal-width confidence bins $B_m$ and returns $\mathrm{ECE}=\sum_m\frac{|B_m|}{N}|\mathrm{acc}(B_m){-}\mathrm{conf}(B_m)|$. ECE is aggregated over the whole pool; under systematic quality variation it can be moderate while calibration collapses on a critical stratum. Let $q(x)\in[0,1]^5$ be a 5-head IQA quality vector (sharpness, brightness, completeness, colour temperature, contrast; supp \S A1), $\qbar(x){=}\tfrac{1}{5}\sum_i q_i(x)$, and partition $[0,1]$ into strata $\Q_\ell{=}\{x:\qbar(x)\in[\tau_{\ell-1},\tau_\ell)\}$. We define \textbf{Quality-Aware Calibration} $\mathrm{QAC}_\ell$ as ECE restricted to $\Q_\ell$, and the \textbf{Quality-Calibration Degradation Index}
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{QCDI} = \mathrm{QAC}_\text{low} - \mathrm{QAC}_\text{high},\quad \qbar_\text{low}{<}0.45,\ \qbar_\text{high}{>}0.50.
\label{eq:qcdi}
\end{equation}
The intermediate band $[0.45,0.50]$ is reserved for the Edge stratum and omitted from QCDI. Positive QCDI means calibration degrades on low-quality inputs; near zero indicates quality-invariant calibration. We also track predictive entropy $H(p){=}{-}\sum_k p_k\log p_k$ and its Spearman correlation $\rho(H,\qbar)$: a quality-aware model has $\rho{<}0$ (more uncertainty on lower-quality inputs).

\subsection{A Taxonomy of Calibration under Quality Shift}
\label{sec:taxonomy}

Three behavioural regimes by QCDI magnitude: \textbf{Quality-Oblivious} ($\mathrm{QCDI}{>}0.10$; LQ ECE $\gg$ HQ ECE; representative: MC Dropout, Deep Ensembles), \textbf{Quality-Fragile} ($0{<}\mathrm{QCDI}{\le}0.10$; mild quality sensitivity; Std~VIB, Focal+LS), and \textbf{Quality-Aware} ($\mathrm{QCDI}{\le}0$ with $\rho{<}-0.15$, see below; our QCTS). Quality-oblivious methods require structural change; quality-fragile methods are fixable with lightweight post-hoc correction.

\textbf{Disambiguating QCDI sign-flips.}
Negative QCDI does not always mean genuine quality-awareness. A method is \emph{genuinely quality-aware} if QCDI${\le}0$ \textbf{and} $\rho(H,\qbar){<}-\rho_\text{noise}$, where the sampling-noise floor at $n{=}2{,}820$ is $\rho_\text{noise}{\approx}0.04$ at $p{=}0.01$ under a permutation null (supp \S A14). We use $-0.15$ as a conservative threshold (well above the noise floor; robust to sweep $[-0.10,-0.20]$). A method is \emph{quality-confused} if QCDI${\le}0$ is driven by HQ ECE inflation with $|\rho|{<}\rho_\text{noise}$ or $\rho{>}0$. In Table~\ref{tab:universality}, ViT-Tiny$+$TS is quality-confused (QCDI${=}{-}0.029$ via HQ ECE $0.036\to0.072$, $\rho$ unchanged); ViT-Tiny$+$QCTS is genuinely quality-aware (QCDI${=}{-}0.017$, $\rho{=}{-}0.266$, stable HQ ECE).

\section{ITB Benchmark and QCTS Method}
\label{sec:method}
%=====================================================================

We describe ITB (\S\ref{sec:itb}, Fig.~\ref{fig:method}a) and QCTS (\S\ref{sec:qcts}, Fig.~\ref{fig:method}b).

\subsection{ITB: Image Triage Benchmark}
\label{sec:itb}

ITB partitions a held-out test pool by $\qbar$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}a) into ITB-LQ ($\qbar{<}0.45$, $n{=}300$, the clinically critical subset), ITB-Edge ($\qbar\in[0.45,0.50]$, $n{=}660$, the band reserved between LQ and HQ in the QCDI definition), ITB-HQ ($\qbar{>}0.50$, $n{=}360$), and ITB-Diverse ($n{=}1{,}500$ FitzPatrick17k images covering skin types I--VI). The contribution is the quality-stratified evaluation \emph{methodology} and the QCDI metric; any labelled image set with an associated quality scalar adopts the same protocol. \textbf{Baselines (7).} EfficientNet-B3 (deterministic); Std~VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib} with prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$; Focal Loss + Label Smoothing; MC Dropout (30 passes, rate~0.2)~\cite{gal2016dropout}; Deep Ensemble (5$\times$ Std~VIB)~\cite{lakshminarayanan2017ensemble}; post-hoc TS~\cite{guo2017calibration} on Std~VIB; Evidential Deep Learning~\cite{sensoy2018evidential}. Architecture probes (ResNet-50, ViT-Tiny, ConvNeXt-Tiny, Swin-Tiny) appear in \S\ref{sec:universality}. \textbf{Datasets.} ISIC~2020 ($33{,}126$, 70/10/20) is the primary corpus; HAM10000~\cite{tschandl2019ham10000} and PAD-UFES~\cite{pacheco2020pad} provide zero-shot transfer pools; programmatic degradation and 5-head IQA scoring yield $149{,}100$ images with 5-dimensional quality annotations.

\subsection{Quality-Conditioned Temperature Scaling}
\label{sec:qcts}

Standard TS learns a single $T\in\R^+$ minimising validation NLL; because $T$ is uniform across inputs, it cannot differentially adjust confidence for degraded images. QCTS replaces $T$ with a continuous function of $\qbar(x)$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}b):
\begin{equation}
T(\qbar) = \mathrm{softplus}\big(T_0 + \alpha (1 - \qbar)\big),\qquad T_0\in\R,\ \alpha\in\R_{\ge 0},
\label{eq:qcts}
\end{equation}
with $\mathrm{softplus}(u){=}\log(1{+}e^u)$ guaranteeing $T{>}0$. Eq.~\ref{eq:qcts} is a \emph{design choice}: we list three inductive biases any quality-conditioned temperature should respect---\emph{(B1) Monotonicity} ($\qbar\mapsto T$ non-increasing, so degraded inputs are softened more), \emph{(B2) Positivity} ($T{>}0$), \emph{(B3) Smoothness and TS reduction} ($T$ smooth in $\qbar$ and recovers TS at $\alpha{=}0$)---which are necessary but not sufficient: infinitely many functions satisfy them. We then ablate among the families that minimally add a quality slope: linear $T_0{+}\alpha(1{-}\qbar)$ violates B2 when $T_0{+}\alpha{<}0$; ReLU breaks B3 at the rectification point; piecewise-constant minimises validation NLL but produces the worst QCDI (Table~\ref{tab:ablation}). Softplus is the smallest-parameter monotone-positive-smooth form we tested; we report results on it and treat it as the simplest defensible default rather than a derived optimum. With $f_\theta$ frozen we fit $(T_0,\alpha)$ by L-BFGS on the same NLL objective as TS, $-\sum_{(x,y)\in\D_\text{val}}\log\mathrm{softmax}(z(x)/T(\qbar(x)))_y$, initialised at zero, up to 200 iterations.

\textbf{Connection to Information Bottleneck.}
Larger $T$ flattens the predictive distribution analogously to a higher VIB~\cite{alemi2017vib} compression $\beta$; QCTS makes this compression quality-dependent ($T(\qbar{=}0){\approx}2.24$ vs.\ $T(\qbar{=}1){\approx}1.44$ on Std~VIB, $56\%$ higher on LQ). A PAC-Bayes motivation~\cite{mcallester1999pac} is in supp \S A22.

\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}
%=====================================================================

Our experiments fix the training and evaluation protocol (\S\ref{sec:setup}), rank seven baselines on ITB (\S\ref{sec:main_results}), isolate QCTS and inspect the learned $T(\qbar)$ (\S\ref{sec:qcts_analysis}), probe universality across backbones (\S\ref{sec:universality}), and verify zero-shot, fairness, and quality-scalar transfer (\S\ref{sec:generalisation}).

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

All models trained on ISIC~2020 (70/10/20). EfficientNet-based models use AdamW with cosine annealing ($\text{lr}{=}10^{-4}$, batch~128, 90~epochs); MC Dropout uses 30 passes at dropout~0.2; Deep Ensemble combines 5 Std~VIB; TS/QCTS L-BFGS for 200 iters. All training fits on a single RTX~4070~8GB. Metrics: AUC; ECE ($M{=}15$); QCDI; Spearman $\rho(H,\qbar)$. Bootstrap with $n{=}10^4$ resamples; metrics averaged over 3 seeds.

\subsection{Main Results and Calibration Taxonomy}
\label{sec:main_results}

The methods with the strongest aggregate ITB performance are the most miscalibrated on low-quality inputs. MC Dropout and Deep Ensemble reach the highest ITB-LQ AUC (0.693, 0.711; Table~\ref{tab:main}) but the worst ECE ($0.615$, $0.440$, ${\approx}3\times$ Std~VIB); both sit in the Quality-Oblivious band (QCDI${>}0.10$). Standard TS lifts Std~VIB's global ECE only slightly and leaves QCDI unchanged ($+0.015$ vs.\ $+0.016$); EDL---the one training-time quality-aware method---carries ECE above $0.27$ on both strata and barely positive $\rho$ ($+0.039$), Quality-Fragile. Only Std~VIB$+$QCTS comes out clearly quality-aware: $\rho{=}-0.249$ ($p{<}10^{-30}$), the strongest negative in Table~\ref{tab:main}; MC Dropout and Deep Ensemble manage only $\rho\approx-0.11$ to $-0.12$. Per-stratum reliability diagrams (supp Fig.~S3, \S A18) make the failure visible: on ITB-LQ MC Dropout drifts substantially below the diagonal while QCTS stays closest to it. Restricting to the ITB-LQ stratum alone, MC Dropout's correlation flips to $\rho{=}+0.350$ ($p{<}10^{-9}$) and Deep Ensemble's to $\rho{=}+0.133$ ($p{=}0.021$): a Simpson-style divergence in which methods that appear marginally quality-aware in aggregate are quality-oblivious in the critical LQ tier.

On Std~VIB---a quality-fragile backbone whose VIB bottleneck intentionally suppresses logit magnitude (ITB-LQ AUC $0.553$, $\rho{=}-0.153$ on the full pool, $n{=}2{,}820$, $p{<}10^{-15}$)---TS produces a deployment-time predictive distribution with $\rho{=}+0.241$ ($p{<}10^{-37}$, Table~\ref{tab:main}). A 3-way forward ablation isolates the source of this flip (supp \S A19): \emph{(a)} MC-marginalised raw forward $\rho_a{=}{-}0.163$; \emph{(b)} raw deterministic-$\mu$ forward (T${=}1$) $\rho_b{=}{+}0.241$; \emph{(c)} deterministic-$\mu$ + scalar TS (T${=}2.32$) $\rho_c{=}{+}0.241$. The MC$\to$deterministic forward swap accounts for $|\Delta\rho_{a\to b}|{=}0.404$ ($100\%$ of the flip); scalar TS contributes $|\Delta\rho_{b\to c}|{<}10^{-5}$, consistent with the fact that scalar TS is monotonic in deterministic logits and cannot change Spearman ranks. The implication is sharper than ``TS is broken'': practitioners ship the deterministic-$\mu$ forward (not MC sampling) and then apply scalar TS that cannot undo the rank inversion introduced by the forward-pass swap. On ViT-Tiny---a strictly deterministic classifier with no MC component---this confound vanishes; TS leaves $\rho$ unchanged but flips QCDI ($+0.023\to-0.029$), isolating the within-forward consequence of the TS objective from the MC-vs-deterministic component that confounds the Std~VIB row. On strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 TS is neutral on both metrics. QCTS keeps QCDI near zero and strengthens negative $\rho$ on every backbone tested (Table~\ref{tab:universality}).

\subsection{QCTS Analysis}
\label{sec:qcts_analysis}

Applied to a frozen Std~VIB backbone, QCTS reduces ECE on ITB-LQ from $0.146$ to $0.079$ ($46\%$ relative) while leaving HQ ECE essentially flat ($0.129\to0.075$): a selective correction that softens low-quality predictions without harming high-quality calibration. QCDI drops from $+0.016$ to $+0.004$ ($75\%$ over Std~VIB, $73\%$ over TS), making QCTS the only post-hoc method in our benchmark to enter the Quality-Aware regime. All three seeds converge to positive $\alpha$ ($0.34, 0.96, 0.37$); profiling $T_0$, the NLL forms a shallow basin around $\alpha\in[0.7,1.1]$ ($\Delta\text{NLL}{<}10^{-4}$ within this band) widening to $\Delta\text{NLL}{<}1.2{\times}10^{-3}$ across $\alpha\in[0.20,1.35]$ (supp \S A17). Two of three seeds ($\alpha{=}0.34,0.37$) sit at the boundary of the wider band rather than at the basin minimum, indicating seed-dependent initialisation rather than true multi-modality; a 10-seed re-run is left to future work. We recommend the median-seed default $\alpha{=}0.37$ for reproducibility despite its sub-optimal NLL, since all three seeds remain in the Quality-Aware regime (QCDI ${\le}0.006$). Table~\ref{tab:ablation} compares softplus against linear and 3-bin piecewise-constant alternatives: piecewise minimises validation NLL but produces the worst QCDI and a near-zero entropy--$\qbar$ correlation, so NLL alone is not a sufficient objective for quality-aware calibration. Five pairwise QCTS-vs-baseline ECE comparisons survive Bonferroni correction (smallest corrected $p{=}0.0015$); at the minimum clinically meaningful ECE reduction of $0.03$, $n{=}117$ achieves $80\%$ power, so $n_\text{LQ}{=}300$ is well-powered (supp \S A16, Attack 3). Per-degradation, QCTS cuts ITB-LQ ECE by $51\%$ on blur and $41\%$ on colour-temperature. \textbf{One failure mode is documented honestly}: on contrast-degraded inputs QCTS \emph{worsens} ECE by $58\%$, because logit-rescaling cannot repair contrast-induced logit spreading---a single scalar $T$ shrinks logits but cannot restore the relative geometry the contrast attack flattened. This is the canonical limit of any post-hoc logit calibrator. Brightness ($-12\%$) is intermediate. Per-$\bar q$ quintile, every quintile sees a $16$--$33\%$ reduction with the largest gain on the lowest 20\% (full breakdown in supp \S A12).

Supp \S A2 tests whether QCTS depends on the 5-head IQA module: BRISQUE~\cite{brisque} and CLIP-IQA~\cite{clipiqa} collapse to $\hat\alpha\approx0$ (equivalent to TS); Laplacian variance learns a non-trivial slope ($\hat\alpha{=}0.95$) but degrades QCDI to $+0.028$. Only the dermoscopy-trained 5-head IQA achieves QCDI${<}0$ and the most negative $\rho$. Compared against quality-stratified Platt scaling~\cite{platt1999probabilistic} and quality-binned isotonic regression (ITB-Edge-fit), both reach QCDI${<}0$ but invert $\rho$ to $+0.195$ and $+0.629$: they compress LQ predictions into narrow confidence bands, falling into the quality-confused regime of \S\ref{sec:taxonomy}. QCTS achieves QCDI${=}{+}0.004$ and $\rho{=}{-}0.226$ simultaneously, the only approach satisfying both criteria (supp \S A10).

\subsection{Universality across Backbone Families}
\label{sec:universality}

We re-fit the same QCTS protocol (frozen backbone, val-set L-BFGS over $T_0, \alpha$) on four additional architectures --- ResNet-50, ViT-Tiny (DeiT init.), ConvNeXt-Tiny, Swin-Tiny --- all trained on ISIC~2020 with the same 70/10/20 split. Table~\ref{tab:universality} reports three families in the main body; ConvNeXt-Tiny and Swin-Tiny in supp \S A13.

\textbf{TS reversal manifests differently across backbones.}
On Std~VIB reversal surfaces as a $\rho$ sign-flip ($-0.153\to+0.241$). On ViT-Tiny TS leaves $\rho$ unchanged but flips the sign of QCDI ($+0.023\to-0.029$): ECE-HQ jumps $0.036\to0.072$ while ECE-LQ drops $0.058\to0.043$. Swin-Tiny mirrors ViT-Tiny ($+0.020\to-0.021$); ConvNeXt-Tiny is milder ($+0.136\to-0.022$). On the strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 ($\rho{=}-0.368$), TS is approximately neutral. Reversal is therefore a property of \emph{weakly} quality-aware backbones; \S\ref{sec:discussion} gives the structural reason.

\textbf{QCTS is consistent on $\rho$, with diminishing returns on QCDI.}
Every QCTS row pushes $\rho$ more negative than its raw counterpart ($\rho_\text{QCTS}$: Std~VIB $-0.249$, ResNet-50 $-0.380$, ViT-Tiny $-0.266$, ConvNeXt-Tiny $-0.270$, Swin-Tiny $-0.259$, all $p{<}10^{-20}$ via $10^4$-resample bootstrap). QCDI gains are confined to weakly quality-aware backbones: on the strongly quality-aware ResNet-50, raw QCDI is already $+0.014$ and QCTS leaves it at $+0.014$ (the room to improve is exhausted), whereas Std~VIB / ViT-Tiny / ConvNeXt move clearly toward zero. Fitted $\alpha$ correlates with raw quality-awareness: ViT-Tiny ($\alpha{=}1.40$) needs the steepest slope, ResNet-50 ($\alpha{=}0.24$) the shallowest. Binning ITB by $\bar q$ into 20 deciles, the fitted softplus tracks per-bin optimal $T^*(\bar q)$ on ViT-Tiny while ResNet-50's curve is much flatter (supp Fig.~S6, \S A23).

\subsection{Generalisation, Fairness, and Cross-Scalar Transfer}
\label{sec:generalisation}

External checks confirm QCTS is not an ISIC artefact. \textbf{Cross-dataset.} On HAM10000 ($n{=}10{,}015$, dermoscopy distribution overlapping but not identical to ISIC) and PAD-UFES ($n{=}2{,}298$, smartphone clinical photographs), Std~VIB$+$QCTS retains a significant entropy--$\qbar$ correlation (HAM $\rho{=}-0.108$, $p{<}10^{-25}$; PAD $\rho{=}-0.150$, $p{<}10^{-12}$) and improves over uncalibrated Std~VIB ($\rho{:}{-}0.033\to{-}0.108$ on HAM, a modest absolute shift on a distribution-shared pool). The TS reversal resurfaces on both unseen pools (HAM $-0.033\to+0.324$; PAD $-0.075\to+0.072$), reproducing the failure mode on independent dermoscopy/photograph data. \textbf{Fitzpatrick.} On ITB-Diverse, MC~Dropout is quality-oblivious across skin types (QCDI $+0.124$ on I--II, $+0.103$ on V--VI). On the V--VI cohort ($n{=}136$), Std~VIB$+$QCTS pushes the entropy--$\qbar$ correlation more negative ($\rho{:}{-}0.134\to{-}0.306$, bootstrap $p{<}10^{-3}$), directionally consistent with the gain on lighter skin types but estimated on a small stratum (type VI alone has $n{=}43$; we omit per-skin QCDI for VI as unreliable, see supp \S A5). \textbf{Quality-scalar transfer.} Substituting ImageNet-C corruption severity for the learned $\qbar$ ($\bar q = 1 - s/5$, $s\in\{1,\ldots,5\}$) on the 300 ITB-LQ images, TS leaves $\rho$ essentially unchanged on all 18 corruptions ($|\Delta\rho|{<}10^{-4}$), while QCTS strengthens $\rho$ across \emph{all 18} for both ResNet-50 (mean $-0.124\to-0.136$) and ViT-Tiny (mean $-0.139\to-0.210$, consistent with ViT-Tiny's $\alpha{=}1.40$). A cross-modality check on chest X-ray (DenseNet-121~\cite{cohen2022torchxrayvision}, intrinsic $\rho{=}-0.971$) shows QCTS gracefully reduces to TS ($\alpha{\approx}0.21$), confirming neutrality on already-quality-aware backbones; on fundus (APTOS 2019~\cite{aptos2019}, raw $\rho{=}+0.259$) the dermoscopy-trained IQA module is mis-aligned and QCTS is inert, a scope condition rather than a contradiction. Full per-corruption, per-skin-type, and cross-modality tables in supp \S A5/A6/A15.

%=====================================================================
\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%=====================================================================

\textbf{Why existing methods are quality-oblivious.}
MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles inject uncertainty at the parameter level: a blurry input leaves parameters unchanged, so dropout noise and ensemble variance follow the same distribution as for clean inputs. Input-dependent uncertainty therefore requires an explicit mechanism that conditions the predictive distribution on input properties. QCTS supplies this in the simplest possible form: temperature as a function of $\qbar$ adds a quality-dependent confidence penalty without modifying internal representations, separating \emph{what to predict} from \emph{how confident to be}. The same separation is also QCTS's ceiling: logit rescaling cannot repair quality-degraded feature extraction.

\textbf{Why the deployable pipeline reverses on weakly quality-aware backbones.}
On Std~VIB the reversal is driven by the forward-pass swap (MC$\to$deterministic-$\mu$, $\Delta\rho{=}+0.404$, supp \S A19), not by the TS objective; scalar TS is monotonic in the deterministic logits and preserves Spearman rank ($\Delta\rho{<}10^{-5}$). On a strictly deterministic backbone (ViT-Tiny, no MC component) the same TS objective acts within the forward and flips the bin-based QCDI ($+0.023\to-0.029$), because ECE is binned and not preserved under scalar $T$; on a strongly quality-aware ResNet-50 the deterministic forward retains enough quality signal that scalar TS is approximately neutral on both metrics. The common cause across these three regimes is the same: the deployable post-hoc pipeline (deterministic-$\mu$ + scalar~$T$) has zero degrees of freedom along $\qbar$ and inherits whatever quality dependence the deterministic forward exposes. Quality-aware calibration therefore needs at least one degree of freedom along $\qbar$; QCTS supplies exactly that via the slope $\alpha$. A $\tau$ sweep (IQA-derived and Laplacian-variance proxies) and a confidently-wrong failure-mode audit (blur 49\% / colour-distorted 32\% / ambiguous 19\%, matching the dimensions QCTS softens most) are in supp \S A9/A8.

\textbf{Limitations.}
\emph{(i) Reversal is a property of weakly quality-aware backbones with a deterministic-$\mu$ deployment forward}, not an architectural law: ResNet-50 absorbs the pipeline without reversal. \emph{(ii) ITB-LQ uses programmatic degradations}; as a partial in-the-wild check, $174$ ISIC 2020 real low-quality dermoscopy images give EfficientNet-B3 ECE $0.073$ $[0.038,0.125]$, below ITB-LQ's $0.146$, but a cross-class evaluation with matched malignant LQ images remains needed (supp \S A7). \emph{(iii) QCTS is bounded above by the IQA module's resolution}: the Fundus null result (\S\ref{sec:generalisation}) illustrates how an out-of-domain IQA collapses the gain. \emph{(iv) ITB uses a binary task}; multi-class ECE semantics differ. \emph{(v) Clinical impact is not established}: decision-curve analysis on ITB-LQ gives Std~VIB$+$QCTS max net benefit $0.192$ [bootstrap $95\%$ CI $0.148, 0.242$] vs.\ Std~VIB $0.186$ [$0.145, 0.233$] (supp \S A3); the confidence intervals overlap, so the absolute net-benefit gap is not statistically distinguishable at $n{=}300$. We deliberately do not claim QCTS is a deployable triage system; a prospective study with matched malignant low-quality images is required for any clinical claim. Estimator robustness (equal-width vs.\ equal-mass binning at $M\in\{10,15\}$; debiased ECE~\cite{kumar2019verified} \emph{enlarges} the QCTS gap; Spearman ties $\sigma_\rho{<}10^{-5}$) is reported in supp \S A20.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%=====================================================================

Medical-AI calibration should be evaluated as a function of input quality, not only in aggregate. We gave the idea three handles---QAC, QCDI, a quality-oblivious/fragile/aware taxonomy---instantiated them on ITB, and proposed QCTS, a two-parameter post-hoc calibrator that cuts QCDI by $73\%$ over standard TS on Std~VIB without retraining and transfers across five backbone families. The consequential finding is that MC Dropout and Deep Ensembles---two popular uncertainty methods---are quality-oblivious on the low-quality inputs that medical triage depends on. Natural next steps: modality-specific IQA modules, a prospective study linking QCDI to triage outcomes, and quality-conditioned variants of richer post-hoc methods.
