\section{Agent Implementation}\label{app:a5}

This appendix documents the implementation of the four-channel routing policy
$\pi^{*}$ of \cref{sec:agent-routing}. We give (i)~the quality-tier routing
procedure with the verifier-frozen thresholds, (ii)~the deterministic
rule-fallback specification used whenever the language model is unavailable or
its proposed action violates the threshold rules, and (iii)~a worked
ReAct-style trace. Throughout, the agent \emph{never} emits a diagnosis: every
channel hands off to the same fixed downstream classifier, and the agent's only
output is the choice of channel together with a short rationale.

\subsection{Quality-tier routing}\label{app:a5-routing}

The controller is a lightweight ReAct-style loop backed by a small
instruction-tuned language model with a deterministic rule fallback. On each
turn it observes the per-dimension quality vector $q\in[0,1]^5$ (and its mean
$\qbar$) returned by the quality assessor and, where required, the predictive
entropy $\Hent(\hat{p}_x)$, and it emits exactly one tool call: assess quality,
enhance, analyse (optionally \emph{cautioned}), ask for re-acquisition, or
finalise. The core dispatch on the quality scalar is the tier function below,
which is the implementation of the quality-driven part of the policy
$\pi^{*}$ in \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body}. The three thresholds are the
grid-search tiers fixed on the validation split and frozen by the numerical
audit: $\taulow=0.25$, $\tauenh=0.35$, $\tauhigh=0.50$.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\fbox{\begin{minipage}{0.94\linewidth}
\textbf{\textsc{RouteByQuality}} --- quality-tier dispatch (mirrors
\texttt{orchestrator.py::route\_by\_quality}; thresholds
$\taulow=0.25,\ \tauenh=0.35,\ \tauhigh=0.50$).\\[2pt]
\textbf{input:} per-input quality scalar $\qbar\in[0,1]$ (mean of the five quality heads)\\
\textbf{output:} channel in $\{\actd,\acte,\actq,\actr\}$ (direct, enhance, query-for-retake, refuse)\\[2pt]
\texttt{if}\ $\qbar \geq \tauhigh$ \ \texttt{then}\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{DirectDiagnosis}
\hfill\emph{(high tier $\qbar\geq0.50$; original image to classifier)}\\
\texttt{else if}\ $\qbar \geq \tauenh$ \ \texttt{then}\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{CautionedDiagnosis}
\hfill\emph{(band $[0.35,0.50)$; direct, uncertainty-annotated)}\\
\texttt{else if}\ $\qbar \geq \taulow$ \ \texttt{then}\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{EnhanceThenDiagnose}
\hfill\emph{(band $[0.25,0.35)$; apply $\Tw$, then diagnose)}\\
\texttt{else}\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{QueryForRetake}
\hfill\emph{(severe tier $\qbar<0.25$; request re-acquisition, do not diagnose)}
\end{minipage}}
\caption{Quality-tier dispatch procedure. The dispatch is on the quality scalar
alone, by design; the predictive-entropy gate is applied on top (see text).}
\label{alg:a5-route}
\end{figure}

\Cref{alg:a5-route} dispatches on the quality scalar alone, by design; the
secondary predictive-entropy gate $\hhigh$ of \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body} is
applied on top of this tier decision. Concretely, a case that
\textsc{RouteByQuality} assigns to the enhance band but whose predictive entropy
exceeds the high-percentile gate $\hhigh$ (a quality-sufficient yet
intrinsically ambiguous lesion that enhancement cannot disambiguate) is escalated
to the refuse action $\actr$ rather than enhanced; symmetrically, a
high-completeness failure ($q_3$ below the completeness floor) routes to
query-for-retake even when $\qbar$ would otherwise admit enhancement. This is the
behaviour formalised by the case split of \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body}, and the
implementation keeps the quality tiering (\Cref{alg:a5-route}) and the entropy
gate as two explicit, auditable steps.

\subsection{Rule-fallback specification}\label{app:a5-fallback}

To guarantee that routing is never less safe than the policy, the controller is
shadowed by a deterministic finite-state fallback that applies
\eqref{eq:agent-policy-body} directly. The fallback is invoked whenever (a)~the
language model cannot be loaded, (b)~the model emits a non-tool or malformed
response, or (c)~the model's proposed action contradicts
\Cref{alg:a5-route} for the observed $\qbar$. The fallback never diagnoses on
its own either; it dispatches to the same downstream classifier as the
language-model path. Its specification is given in \Cref{alg:a5-fallback}.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\fbox{\begin{minipage}{0.94\linewidth}
\textbf{\textsc{RuleFallback}} --- deterministic routing state machine (mirrors
\texttt{orchestrator.py::\_RuleBasedFallback}); $\rho_{\max}$ is the maximum
number of re-acquisition rounds before a cautioned bottom-out.\\[2pt]
\textbf{input:} image $x$; running re-acquisition counter $r$; round cap $\rho_{\max}$\\[2pt]
$q \gets \textsc{QualityAssess}(x)$; \quad $c \gets \textsc{RouteByQuality}(\bar q)$
\hfill\emph{($\bar q$ is the mean of $q$)}\\
\texttt{if}\ $c = \textsc{QueryForRetake}$:\\
\quad \texttt{if}\ $r \geq \rho_{\max}$:\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{Analyse}$(x,\ \text{cautioned}{=}\text{true})$
\hfill\emph{(bottom-out after round cap)}\\
\quad \texttt{else}:\ $r \gets r+1$;\ emit re-acquisition question for the top quality issue;\ \textbf{wait for user}
\hfill\emph{(collection loop, not abstain/defer)}\\
\texttt{else if}\ $c = \textsc{EnhanceThenDiagnose}$:\ $x' \gets \Tw(x)$;\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{Analyse}$(x',\ \text{cautioned}{=}\text{false})$
\hfill\emph{(enhance, then diagnose)}\\
\texttt{else if}\ $c = \textsc{CautionedDiagnosis}$:\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{Analyse}$(x,\ \text{cautioned}{=}\text{true})$
\hfill\emph{(diagnose on original, annotated)}\\
\texttt{else}\ ($c = \textsc{DirectDiagnosis}$):\ \textbf{return}\ \textsc{Analyse}$(x,\ \text{cautioned}{=}\text{false})$
\end{minipage}}
\caption{Deterministic rule-fallback routing state machine. It dispatches to the
same downstream classifier as the language-model path and never diagnoses on its
own.}
\label{alg:a5-fallback}
\end{figure}

Two safety properties follow directly from the construction. First, the severe
tier ($\qbar<\taulow$) always enters the re-acquisition loop until either the
quality clears the tier on a re-captured image or the round cap $\rho_{\max}$ is
reached, at which point the case bottoms out to a \emph{cautioned} diagnosis
rather than a silent direct one. Second, the enhance action is reachable only
within the moderate band, so the restorer $\Tw$ is never applied to severe-tier
inputs --- the regime in which our boundary evidence shows enhancement harms the
diagnosis (\cref{sec:exp-boundary}). The restorer call degrades gracefully: if
the enhancement checkpoint is unavailable at run time the implementation falls
back to the original image rather than failing the case.

\subsection{Worked ReAct trace}\label{app:a5-trace}

\Cref{tab:a5-trace} traces the controller on a moderately degraded input
($\qbar=0.31$, falling in the lower-moderate band $[\taulow,\tauenh)$). The
controller reads the quality vector, routes to the enhance-then-diagnose
channel, hands the enhanced image to the downstream classifier, and finalises
with a referral recommendation --- without ever issuing a diagnosis of its own.

For contrast, a severe-tier input ($\qbar<\taulow$) diverges at Step~2: the
controller emits \texttt{ask\_user($\cdot$)} with a re-acquisition question
targeted at the top quality issue, enters the \textbf{wait-for-user} state, and
re-runs \texttt{quality\_assess()} on the re-captured image, repeating until the
quality clears the severe tier or the round cap $\rho_{\max}$ is reached. This is
the query-for-retake channel: the agent asks for a better image rather than
abstaining or deferring the case to a human.
