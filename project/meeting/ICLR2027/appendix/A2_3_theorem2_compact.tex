\begin{theorem}[Closed-Loop Agent Risk Bound, informal]\label{thm:agent-compact}
Let $\Tw$ be a diagnosis-preserving enhancement satisfying Proposition~\ref{prop:entropy}
with calibration error $\delta_{\TV}$. Under the threshold policy \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body},
the agent's expected risk obeys
\begin{equation}
\Ragent(x) \;\leq\; \Rdirect(x) - \Delta(\qbar,\Tw),
\qquad
\Delta(\qbar,\Tw) > 0 \iff \qbar\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh].
\label{eq:thm2-body}
\end{equation}
In particular the agent is never worse than direct diagnosis, and strictly better on the
salvage band with population gain $\pisalv\cdot\Delta_{\mathrm{avg}}$. The full proof, threshold
estimation protocol, and finite-sample concentration are deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:a2-thm2}.
\end{theorem}
