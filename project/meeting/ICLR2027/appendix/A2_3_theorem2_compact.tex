\begin{theorem}[Closed-Loop Agent Local Risk Bound, informal]\label{thm:agent-compact}
Let $\Tw$ be a diagnosis-preserving enhancement satisfying Lemma~\ref{lem:dp} with calibration
error $\delta_{\TV}$. Under the threshold policy \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body}, we derive that
\emph{within the salvage band} $\qbar\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh]$ enhance-then-diagnose attains a
strictly lower conditional expected risk than direct diagnosis,
\begin{equation}
\Renh(x) \;\leq\; \Rdirect(x) - \Delta(\qbar,\Tw),
\qquad
\Delta(\qbar,\Tw) > 0 \;\text{ for }\; \qbar\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh],
\label{eq:thm2-body}
\end{equation}
with the per-band risk reductions realised empirically (CIs excluding zero in every band,
\Cref{sec:exp-salvage}). This is a \emph{local} statement under the band condition; we make
\emph{no} claim that the agent's risk is globally below direct diagnosis. On the contrary, at
a fixed referral budget our triage simulation finds the global routed policy
\emph{does not} outperform direct diagnosis (Direct triage sensitivity $0.818$ versus the
agent's $0.788$, and statistically indistinguishable net benefit; \Cref{sec:exp-dca}). Closing
the gap between the local gain and global utility --- by learning the routing thresholds rather
than fixing them by grid search --- is left to future work. The full proof, threshold
estimation protocol, and finite-sample concentration are in Appendix~\ref{app:a2-thm2}.
\end{theorem}
