\begin{proposition}[Variance-Driven Entropy Term, Directional]\label{prop:entropy}
Under Assumptions A1--A4 (quality improvement, calibrated scoring, monotone prior,
diagnostic preservation), for $x$ in the salvage band and $q'=f_\eta(\Tw(x,q))$, the
variance-driven component of the expected predictive entropy obeys the directional bound
\begin{equation}
\E_{x}\!\big[\Hent(\hat{p}_{\Tw(x,q)})\big] \;\leq\;
\E_{x}\!\big[\Hent(\hat{p}_x)\big]
- \tfrac{d}{2}\log\tfrac{\sigma^2(\qbar)}{\sigma^2(\qbar')} + 2\beta\sqrt{\epsilon},
\label{eq:prop3}
\end{equation}
\emph{provided} the unmodelled mean-drift term is non-positive in expectation. The first
negative term is the \qvib{} variance gap (Lemma~1, since $\qbar'>\qbar \Rightarrow \sigma^2$
shrinks) and $2\beta\sqrt{\epsilon}$ bounds the magnitude of the diagnostic-preservation slack
(Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}). We emphasise that the proviso is \textbf{not} empirically supported (a
quality-conditioned probe finds the net predictive entropy \emph{increases} after enhancement);
\eqref{eq:prop3} should therefore be read as characterising the sign of the $\sigma^2$-driven
term, not as a net entropy reduction. The information-preservation role of this result in the
paper is carried instead by Lemma~\ref{lem:dp} (validated by experiment E7) and the Stage-1
PSNR$\,\geq 30$\,dB non-emptiness condition; see Appendix~\ref{app:a2-prop3} and the main-text
Limitations.
\end{proposition}

\begin{lemma}[Diagnosis-Preserving Mutual-Information Bound]\label{lem:dp}
Let $q_\theta$ be $\Lq$-Lipschitz in total variation and let the label entropy be
bounded by $M=\log K$. If $\Ldp := \KL(p_\phi^{\mathrm{enh}} \,\|\, p_\phi^{\mathrm{ref}}) \leq \epsilon$, then
\begin{equation}
I(\Zenh; Y) \;\geq\; I(\Zref; Y) - \beta\sqrt{\epsilon},
\qquad \beta = \frac{M\,\Lq}{\sqrt{2}}.
\label{eq:lemma3}
\end{equation}
The $\sqrt{\epsilon}$ rate is the standard Pinsker scaling for a KL budget; a linear-$\epsilon$
rate would require a stronger $\chi^2$-divergence budget (Appendix~A2.2.5). The derivation
combines Pinsker's inequality with a Lipschitz coupling argument (Appendix~A2.2); we state it
as a derivation under the listed assumptions rather than a fully watertight proof.
\end{lemma}
