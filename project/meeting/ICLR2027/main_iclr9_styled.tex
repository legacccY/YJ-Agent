\title{Closed-Loop Quality-Triage for Reliable Skin-Lesion Diagnosis\\
on Quality-Varying Consumer Imagery}
\author{Anonymous Authors}

\begin{document}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We present a closed-loop quality-triage system for skin-lesion diagnosis on degraded consumer
imagery, which decides, per input, whether to diagnose directly, to apply diagnosis-preserving
enhancement, or to query for a retake, instead of passively enhancing every image or blindly
deferring. The decision is driven by a per-input quality estimate; in our instantiation a five-head
assessor (\visiscore) scores perceptual quality with PLCC $0.924$ / SRCC $0.895$, though any
no-reference quality scalar can take its place. Our first contribution is a recipe for enhancing
\emph{safely}: a feature-level diagnosis-preserving objective (DP-Loss) whose information loss we bound
by a Pinsker-optimal mutual-information lower bound (Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}). Empirically, within a
moderate-degradation window enhancement leaves diagnosis essentially unchanged ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=
-0.012$, $95.8\%$ enhanced-versus-reference agreement, McNemar $p=0.57$), and the DP-Loss ablation
confirms its effect ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}$ improves by $+0.021$, $95\%$ CI
$[+0.005,+0.035]$; McNemar $p=2.3\times10^{-45}$). To map \emph{when} enhancement is licensed we chart
a reliability~$\times$~recoverability decision surface (per-dimension~$\times$~per-severity, AUC-led),
on which blur is the most fragile dimension ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.091$ at the most severe setting)
and extreme contrast loss is the one regime where enhancement is significantly harmful
($-0.036$, $95\%$ CI excluding zero). Outside the safe window the system requests a retake --- to our
knowledge the first re-acquisition channel in this setting, rather than mere abstention --- governed by
a local, per-band conditional risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}). We also report,
rather than hide, an honest negative: under a fixed-threshold policy, global triage does not yet beat
direct diagnosis on confidence-gated sensitivity ($0.818$ for direct diagnosis versus $0.788$ for the
strongest variant at a $20\%$ referral budget), which we read as motivation for learned routing
thresholds. Code and benchmark will be released.
\end{abstract}
