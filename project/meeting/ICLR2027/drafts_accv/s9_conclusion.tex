\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
We presented a closed-loop quality-triage system for skin-lesion diagnosis on degraded
consumer imagery, built on two contributions: a feature-level diagnosis-preserving
enhancement objective (DP-Loss) whose information loss is bounded by a mutual-information
lower bound (Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}) and confirmed empirically, and a query-for-retake channel
that --- to our knowledge, the first to request re-acquisition rather than merely abstain
or defer --- closes the acquisition loop under a local, per-band conditional risk guarantee
(Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}). We also chart its limits: enhancement turns
net-negative on extreme contrast loss and on malignant lesions under severe degradation,
and global triage does not yet beat direct diagnosis --- honest negatives we read as
motivation for learned routing thresholds. Mapping where degradation-aware enhancement
helps, and where it must yield to re-acquisition, is the more useful contribution for
deploying diagnosis on the quality-varying inputs that curated benchmarks exclude by
design.
