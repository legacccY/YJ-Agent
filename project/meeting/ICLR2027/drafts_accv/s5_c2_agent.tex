\section{Query-for-Retake Agent}\label{sec:agent}

The restorer of \cref{sec:enhance} is only safe to apply \emph{within} the salvage band. Outside
it, enhancement either does nothing useful or, as our boundary evidence shows
(\cref{sec:exp-boundary}), harms the diagnosis. The agent therefore does not diagnose: from a
per-input quality reading it \emph{decides} whether to diagnose directly, to enhance and then
diagnose, or to ask for a better image. The last channel is the mechanistic contribution of this
section. Existing reliability mechanisms (selective prediction, learning-to-defer, and the agentic
pipeline AT-CXR; \cref{sec:related}) stop the moment an input is judged unsafe, abstaining or
deferring without acting on the cause of the failure --- a recoverably bad acquisition. The
query-for-retake channel instead closes a \emph{collection} loop back to the sensor, requesting a
better image rather than abstaining on or deferring the present one.

\paragraph{Four-channel routing.}\label{sec:agent-routing}
The policy $\pi^{*}$ reads a per-input quality scalar $\qbar(x)$ --- learned here by \visiscore{},
but in principle any per-input quality estimate (a no-reference IQA score, or a known corruption
severity) --- together with a predictive-entropy gate $\Hent(\hat p_x)\!\le\!\hhigh$, and selects
one of four actions $\{\actd,\acte,\actq,\actr\}$:
\begin{equation}
\pi^{*}(x)=
\begin{cases}
\actd & \qbar(x)\geq\tauhigh \quad\text{(direct diagnosis)},\\[1pt]
\acte & \qbar(x)\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh],\ \Hent(\hat p_x)\leq\hhigh \quad\text{(enhance-then-diagnose)},\\[1pt]
\actq & \taulow\leq\qbar(x)<\tauenh \quad\text{(query-for-retake)},\\[1pt]
\actr & \qbar(x)<\taulow\ \text{or}\ \big(\qbar(x)\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh],\ \Hent(\hat p_x)>\hhigh\big) \quad\text{(cautioned)},
\end{cases}
\label{eq:agent-policy-body}
\end{equation}
with grid-searched thresholds $\taulow\!=\!0.25<\tauenh\!=\!0.35<\tauhigh\!=\!0.50$. The entropy
gate separates a \emph{cautioned} high-quality case, quality-sufficient yet intrinsically ambiguous
and beyond what enhancement can salvage, from one the enhancement pathway can help. The
\textbf{query-for-retake} channel makes this mechanism concrete: instead of abstaining
\citep{geifman2017selective} or deferring the case, the agent issues a \emph{re-acquisition
request} that closes a collection loop around the diagnostic model. Image-quality work that flags
re-capture \citep{vodrahalli2021teledermatology,jalaboi2023explainable} decouples the flag from the
diagnostic objective and offers no risk analysis; here the re-acquisition decision is made jointly
with the diagnostic risk and embedded in the risk-bounded routing policy below. To our knowledge,
this is the first \emph{risk-bounded re-acquisition loop coupled jointly with the diagnostic
decision} in this setting --- a scope we delimit deliberately. The agent never emits a
diagnosis on its own; each channel hands the original, restored, or re-acquired image to the same
downstream classifier.
The uncertainty signal is taken off the shelf, so routing does not depend on any bespoke
quality-conditioned bottleneck.\footnote{A quality-conditioned bottleneck variant was evaluated as
an uncertainty source but showed no measurable advantage over the standard variant here.}
Implementation is a lightweight ReAct-style controller backed by a small language model with a
deterministic rule fallback applying \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body}, so routing is never less safe than
the rule (prompt, fallback specification, and a worked trace in Appendix~\ref{app:a5}). The retake
channel fires where it should, rising monotonically as quality falls across the high-quality,
moderate, and severe bands (\cref{sec:exp-boundary}).

\paragraph{A local, per-band risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}).}\label{sec:agent-thm2}
\emph{Within} the salvage band, and under a diagnosis-preserving restorer satisfying the information
lower bound of \cref{sec:enhance}, we derive that enhance-then-diagnose attains a strictly lower
conditional expected risk than direct diagnosis,
$\Renh(x)\le\Rdirect(x)-\Delta(\qbar(x),\Tw)$ with $\Delta(\qbar,\Tw)>0$ for
$\qbar\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh]$; the per-band reductions are realised empirically with bootstrap $95\%$
CIs excluding zero (\cref{sec:exp-boundary}). The decision-theoretic setup, auxiliary lemmas, and
finite-sample companion are in Appendix~\ref{app:a2-thm2}.

This is deliberately a \emph{local} result: it certifies enhancement as the risk-reducing action
exactly where the band holds, and is silent elsewhere. \textbf{We make no claim that the routed agent
dominates direct diagnosis globally.} The within-band gain does not aggregate to a global ordering,
because the query channel's safety relies on a re-acquisition operator that improves quality only in
expectation, and the thresholds are fixed by grid search. Consistently, our triage simulation reports
an honest \emph{negative} global result (\cref{sec:exp-dca}): at a fixed referral budget direct triage
attains the higher auto-handled sensitivity, with net benefit statistically indistinguishable across
variants. We therefore treat the global utility of routing as an open, empirically negative question,
and leave \emph{learning} the thresholds to future work. The band-local statement is moreover a
population result over the salvage-band subpopulation; it does \emph{not} certify a per-class
reduction, and the malignant subgroup, whose net-negative salvage is our hardest boundary evidence
(\cref{sec:exp-boundary}), stays in the query-for-retake regime rather than the enhance regime.
