\centering
\caption{\textbf{Conditioning, fidelity, and speed ablations.}
Quality-conditional FiLM is a diagnostic, not a perceptual, mechanism: removing it leaves
fidelity essentially unchanged ($\le0.4$\,dB PSNR) yet degrades diagnostic preservation on
every axis. A heavier cross-attention module ($+1.8$M parameters) is statistically
indistinguishable from FiLM (paired-bootstrap CIs all include zero, McNemar $p{=}0.679$),
so we keep FiLM on parsimony grounds. The PSNR\,$=32.74$\,dB clears the $30$\,dB
non-vacuity gate of Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}. Lower is better for $\Delta$AUC magnitude towards
zero and KL; higher is better for agreement, PSNR, SSIM. Best diagnostic-preservation
value in bold. Single-seed; the FiLM-vs-cross-attention comparison is on matched
paired-bootstrap resamples (CIs in text).}
\label{tab:ablations}
