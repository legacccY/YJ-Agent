\section{Experiments}\label{sec:exp}

We evaluate on the \itb{} dermoscopy benchmark, probing diagnostic content with a
frozen EfficientNet-B3 classifier kept \emph{independent} of the enhancer to avoid
circularity. Unless noted, every quantity carries a bootstrap $2000$-sample $95\%$
confidence interval, and significance means the CI excludes zero.

\subsection{The Reliability\,$\times$\,Recoverability Decision Surface}
\label{sec:exp-surface}
We first chart a reliability\,$\times$\,recoverability surface over a $5\times5$ grid
(five degradation dimensions $\times$ five severity levels, $n=360$/cell). The primary
axis reports diagnostic AUC under degradation; a second axis reports
$\Delta_{\text{rec}}=\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}-\mathrm{AUC}$, asking whether enhancement
buys the diagnosis back. This AUC-led, per-dimension $\times$ per-severity view is
distinct from the per-dimension calibration analysis of \citet{ANON-BMVC} (cited, not
reused), which neither sweeps severity nor includes completeness. \textbf{Reliability:}
AUC degrades near-monotonically with severity on every axis, but blur is by far the most
fragile (drop $-0.0911$ from S1 to S5) while contrast looks most robust (only $-0.0153$);
full per-cell values are in \cref{app:surface}. \textbf{Recoverability:} the surface
splits into three regimes (\cref{fig:c0-surface}). Enhancement \emph{reliably recovers}
blur and colour shift from severity S3 onward (CIs excluding zero), is
\emph{statistically inconclusive} on completeness (every level straddles zero, so we call
it partially recoverable rather than irreversible), and \emph{significantly hurts}
exactly one of all $25$ cells: contrast at the extreme level ($\Delta_{\text{rec}}=-0.0355$,
$95\%$ CI $[-0.0783,-0.0013]$). Contrast thus looks robust yet is recoverable in name
only, so the gate must be dimension-aware. This single contrast-S5 cell is one
per-dimension trigger that complements, rather than replaces, the per-class evidence of
\cref{sec:exp-boundary}; we do \emph{not} generalise to ``enhancement is unsafe under
severe degradation''.

\subsection{Diagnostic Preservation}\label{sec:exp-preserve}
On the moderate band the feature-level diagnosis-preserving model leaves diagnosis
essentially intact: $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.012$ (within the $1.5\%$ target),
$95.8\%$ enhanced-versus-reference agreement, and a McNemar test that finds no
significant difference between enhanced and reference predictions ($p=0.573$). The
enhanced inputs are therefore diagnostically interchangeable with their clean
references on aggregate AUC. Preservation is nonetheless imperfect at the level of
individual malignant lesions; we treat the residual dangerous flips (\cref{sec:exp-sota})
as the empirical case for the query-for-retake channel. To forestall a reading of the
two results as contradictory, we state the scope explicitly: this diagnosis-preserving
claim is strictly confined to the \emph{moderate-degradation window} measured on
\emph{aggregate AUC}, whereas the malignant per-class boundary of \cref{sec:exp-boundary}
is exactly what motivates the query-for-retake gate. Aggregate preservation within the
window and net-negative salvage on the malignant subgroup are therefore not in tension
but a two-regime division of labour --- enhance where the band holds, re-acquire where
it does not.

\subsection{DP-Loss Ablation and the Mutual-Information Bound}\label{sec:exp-dploss}
Ablating the diagnosis-preserving objective \eqref{eq:dploss} against an otherwise
identical enhancer trained without it shows DP-Loss is what preserves diagnosis: on
matched bootstrap resamples it raises the diagnostic $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}$
by $+0.0205$ (CI excluding zero), lowers the diagnostic KL by $-0.148$ (CI excluding
zero), and yields McNemar $p=2.3\times10^{-45}$. This is the empirical counterpart of
the mutual-information lower bound in Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}: tightening the feature-level
KL budget measurably raises the diagnostic information retained, the direction the bound
predicts. Together with the Stage-1 PSNR$\,\geq30$\,dB non-vacuity condition, this
carries the information-preservation claim of \cref{sec:prop3lemma3}; we do \emph{not}
rely on a net entropy-reduction claim, which the data do not support.

\subsection{Comparison with Generic Restoration SOTA}\label{sec:exp-sota}
Against six off-the-shelf non-diffusion restoration models applied zero-shot,
\visienhance{} is the only method that both restores fidelity and preserves diagnosis
(\cref{tab:e10}): every generic model is a significantly worse diagnostic preserver
(paired $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}$ CIs all excluding zero, McNemar $p<10^{-150}$), and the
trade-off frontier is plotted in \cref{fig:enhancement}A. This comparison is run under a
\emph{matched zero-shot protocol}, in which no method --- ours included --- is adapted to the
degraded dermoscopy distribution; the gap is therefore the diagnostic-preservation gap between the
objectives, not an artefact of one model having seen the target degradations. To probe whether the
gap survives when each generic baseline is itself fine-tuned on degraded dermoscopy, we report a
matched fine-tuned comparison (\todo{R1 核}) in \cref{sec:exp-transfer}; the present claim is
scoped to the zero-shot protocol. Aggregate preservation does
not, however, mean every lesion is safe: enhancement still pushes a small subset of
correctly-called melanomas below the decision threshold --- a \emph{dangerous flip}
(\cref{fig:enhancement}C) --- which we report transparently as motivation for the
query-for-retake channel (\cref{sec:agent}).

\subsection{DP-Loss is Transferable}\label{sec:exp-transfer}
The preceding comparison establishes that \visienhance{} preserves diagnosis better than generic
restoration, but leaves open whether the advantage comes from the diagnosis-preserving objective
itself or from our particular network. To separate the two, we graft DP-Loss onto a strong generic
restoration backbone, leaving its architecture and capacity unchanged, and re-evaluate diagnostic
preservation under the same protocol; we further fine-tune each generic baseline on degraded
dermoscopy so that the comparison no longer credits \visienhance{} for adaptation alone
(\cref{sec:exp-sota}). Concretely, we test whether grafting DP-Loss onto a strong baseline
restoration backbone reproduces the diagnosis-preserving effect (\todo{R3 核 $\Delta$AUC}, $95\%$ CI
\todo). If the grafted backbone recovers diagnostic preservation, the diagnosis-preserving behaviour
is attributable to the objective rather than to our architecture, and DP-Loss is a portable loss any
restoration backbone can adopt; if instead the grafted backbone does not separate from its
DP-Loss-free counterpart, the advantage of \visienhance{} is attributable to the diagnosis-preserving
objective in combination with our architecture rather than to either alone, and we make no
portability claim. The per-class picture is reported in full and not aggregated away: on the
malignant subgroup the grafted backbone yields a melanoma salvage of \todo{R3 核} (\todo). Should the
grafted backbone remain net-negative on melanoma, we attribute the residual failure to the
restoration boundary itself rather than to any single model, consistent with the per-class evidence
of \cref{sec:exp-boundary}, which we do not aggregate away. The direction of the effect, and hence
the verbs above, are fixed only once the runs complete. All quantities in this subsection are pending
the fine-tuning and grafting runs and are reported only once verified against the result tables
(\todo{R1/R3 核 — HPC 排队中，未填即占位}).

\subsection{Conditioning, Fidelity, and Speed Ablations}\label{sec:exp-ablations}
Quality-conditional FiLM is a diagnostic, not a perceptual, mechanism: removing it
leaves PSNR within $0.4$\,dB ($32.74$ with versus $33.06$ without) yet degrades
diagnostic preservation on every axis (with FiLM $\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.033$, agreement
$0.90$, KL $0.24$, versus $-0.042$, $0.87$, $0.35$ without). Replacing FiLM with a
heavier cross-attention conditioning module ($+1.8$M parameters) is statistically
indistinguishable on every axis (paired bootstrap CIs all include zero, McNemar
$p=0.679$), so we keep FiLM on parsimony grounds. The deterministic enhancer attains
per-image PSNR $32.74$\,dB (SSIM $0.91$, $n=19878$), clearing the $30$\,dB non-vacuity
gate of Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}, and runs end-to-end at $16.08$\,ms/image, within the
real-time budget. A per-degradation breakdown (\cref{fig:enhancement}B) confirms the
enhancer is strongest on brightness and blur but genuinely \emph{harmful} on contrast,
consistent with the contrast-S5 trigger of \cref{sec:exp-surface}.

\subsection{The Honest Boundary: When Enhancement Hurts Diagnosis}
\label{sec:exp-boundary}
A per-class split (\cref{fig:boundary-agent}A) is where the salvage story collapses. The
benign and malignant subgroups behave in opposite directions: enhancement salvages most
degradation-misclassified benign lesions ($75.6\%$, $1809/2392$) while damaging almost
none, yet on melanoma it salvages only $5.2\%$ and damages far more than it rescues --- a
\emph{net loss of malignant cases}, helping the benign errors that least need help and
harming the malignant cases that matter most. We report the two melanoma rates on
distinct conditional pools by construction: the salvage rate is computed over the
\emph{salvageable pool} of melanomas already misclassified after degradation
($n=77$), of which enhancement recovers $4$ ($4/77=5.2\%$), while the damage rate is
computed over the \emph{at-risk pool} of melanomas still correctly called after
degradation ($n=274$), of which enhancement pushes $85$ below the decision threshold
($85/274=31.0\%$); the two denominators differ because the two events are conditioned on
opposite pre-enhancement outcomes, not because cases were selected. Across all $351$
melanomas this leaves a net change of $4-85=-81$ correctly-called malignant cases.
Because the benign subgroup dominates the population, the benign-dominated aggregate
moderate-band salvage rate ($0.737$) is therefore \emph{not} a meaningful capability
number, and we deliberately do not report it as one; only the per-class breakdown
reflects clinical risk. This is not a reconstruction-loss artefact: retraining
with a mask-weighted objective ($4\times$ lesion weight) leaves melanoma salvage
unchanged at $5.2\%$ (an honest null; \cref{fig:boundary-agent}A), so the failure mode
lies beyond what per-pixel $L_1$ can reach. The boundary is sharper still on the severe
band, where enhancement significantly \emph{lowers} diagnostic AUC
($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.0559$, $95\%$ CI $[-0.085,-0.028]$). The agent accordingly routes severe and high-risk inputs to
query-for-retake rather than enhance, with the retake fraction rising strictly with
degradation across bands (\cref{fig:boundary-agent}C), realising the threshold-window
policy of Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}. These per-class, per-severity, and
per-dimension triggers are the hardest motivation for the gate, but they are
\emph{local, per-band} statements only; the honest global comparison is in
\cref{sec:exp-triage}.

\subsection{Cost--Benefit, Decision Curves, and the Global Triage Negative Result}
\label{sec:exp-triage}
\label{sec:exp-dca}% upstream \cref{sec:exp-dca} (rem:no-global) resolves here
On \itb{}-LQ ($n=300$, the worst-case low-quality subset) we run net-benefit
decision-curve analysis and a confidence-gated triage simulation
(\cref{fig:boundary-agent}B; cost model in \cref{app:a20}). The triage gate needs only an
off-the-shelf per-input uncertainty signal; a standard variational information
bottleneck suffices.\footnote{The quality-conditional variant we also report carries
\emph{no} method-gain claim; it appears only as the strongest tested configuration, and
it still does not outperform direct diagnosis.} \textbf{Net benefit does not separate
the methods}: across the clinically relevant threshold range the maximum net benefit is
statistically indistinguishable among direct diagnosis and all bottleneck variants
($0.179$--$0.192$, all $95\%$ CIs overlapping), with the only positive statement being
that each method stays above the treat-all reference. \textbf{The global triage
comparison is an honest negative}: at a $20\%$ referral budget direct diagnosis attains
the \emph{highest} auto-handled sensitivity ($0.818$), the strongest bottleneck variant
only tracks it ($0.788$, not exceeding it), and under-confident variants collapse.
Global quality-aware triage therefore does not outperform direct diagnosis, and the
agent's global risk is not guaranteed to be at most that of direct diagnosis. This is the
honest global counterpart of the band-local guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}
bounds risk only \emph{within} the moderate window, Remark~\ref{rem:no-global}): it
delimits where the system should defer to re-acquisition rather than assert a diagnosis.
The defensible positive reading is narrow: a calibrated posterior supports reliable
confidence-driven abstention, and closing the band-local-to-global gap by \emph{learning}
the routing thresholds is left to future work.
