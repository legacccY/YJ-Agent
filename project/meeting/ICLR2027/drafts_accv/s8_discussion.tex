\section{Discussion and Limitations}\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{Relation to gradient-repair and generative restoration.}
As detailed in \cref{sec:related}, our contribution is orthogonal to GradProm: where
GradProm repairs gradients during training, we \emph{derive} a diagnosis-preserving
information lower bound (\cref{lem:dp}) \emph{and} add a routing decision with a
query-for-retake channel---complementary, not competing. We avoid diffusion- and GAN-based
restoration because optimising perceptual realism can synthesise plausible-but-absent
structure (pigment networks, vessels), a medical-safety hazard; deterministic restoration
instead trades sharpness for an information-preservation property a generative model cannot
guarantee, and diffusion enters only as a rejected cautionary contrast.

\paragraph{What the method establishes, and its honest boundary.}
The positive contribution is concrete and statistically attributable: within the
moderate-degradation window the diagnosis-preserving objective lets \visienhance retain the
discriminative information a frozen probe relies on (DP-Loss ablation
$\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}=+0.0205$, $95\%$ CI $[+0.005,+0.035]$; McNemar
$p=2.3\times10^{-45}$), and it preserves diagnosis better than six restoration baselines on
every one under a matched zero-shot protocol (the fine-tuned comparison is reported in
\cref{sec:exp-transfer}). We are equally explicit about where the method stops: we make no
claim that quality-aware triage beats direct diagnosis \emph{globally}, since at a fixed
threshold direct diagnosis attains the best auto-handled sensitivity ($0.818$ versus
$0.788$ for the strongest variant at a $20\%$ referral budget) with net-benefit CIs
overlapping (\cref{sec:exp-triage}). The positive claim is local, and recoverability is
dimension- and severity-dependent (\cref{sec:exp-surface})---exactly what the
query-for-retake gate is built to exploit.

\paragraph{Limitations and future work.}
Several limitations are themselves the motivation for the retake gate, stated plainly.
(1)~The theory is argued under explicit assumptions rather than watertight, and
\cref{thm:agent-compact} is band-local, not a global ordering.
(2)~Degradations are semi-synthetic; prospective validation on genuinely degraded clinical
captures remains open.
(3)~Routing depends on a per-input quality scalar whose annotation and re-fitting cost we
do not eliminate, with claims restricted to dermatology.
(4)~We report no clinical deployment, and diagnosis-preservation results are tied to a
single frozen probe.
(5)~Enhancement is uneven across axes, backfiring on contrast and underperforming on
colour-shift (\cref{sec:exp-ablations}).
(6)~The malignant-salvage gap is unresolved: enhancement salvages only $5.2\%$ of
misclassified melanomas (a net loss of malignant cases), unchanged by a mask-weighted
retrain, and closing it is the central open question. These negative results are genuine
evidence that the retake gate is a necessary safety mechanism, not a project shortcoming.
