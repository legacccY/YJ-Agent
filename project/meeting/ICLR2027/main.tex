\begin{abstract}
% LOCKED hook (anti-drift rule 1 / R14): first sentence MUST stay on the closed-loop

% Do NOT reintroduce the retired headline number or any global-superiority triage claim.
We present a closed-loop quality-triage system for skin-lesion diagnosis on degraded consumer
imagery, which decides, per input, whether to diagnose directly, to apply diagnosis-preserving
enhancement, or to query for a retake, instead of passively enhancing every image or blindly
deferring. The decision is driven by a per-input quality estimate; in our instantiation a five-head
assessor (\visiscore) scores perceptual quality with PLCC $0.924$ / SRCC $0.895$, though any
no-reference quality scalar can take its place. Our first contribution is a recipe for enhancing
\emph{safely}: a feature-level diagnosis-preserving objective (DP-Loss) whose information loss we bound
by a Pinsker-optimal mutual-information lower bound (Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}). Empirically, within a
moderate-degradation window enhancement leaves diagnosis essentially unchanged ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=
-0.012$, $95.8\%$ enhanced-versus-reference agreement, McNemar $p=0.57$), and the DP-Loss ablation
confirms its effect ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}$ improves by $+0.021$, $95\%$ CI
$[+0.005,+0.035]$; McNemar $p=2.3\times10^{-45}$). To map \emph{when} enhancement is licensed we chart
a reliability~$\times$~recoverability decision surface (per-dimension~$\times$~per-severity, AUC-led),
on which blur is the most fragile dimension ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.091$ at the most severe setting)
and extreme contrast loss is the one regime where enhancement is significantly harmful
($-0.036$, $95\%$ CI excluding zero). Outside the safe window the system requests a retake --- to our
knowledge the first re-acquisition channel in this setting, rather than mere abstention --- governed by
a local, per-band conditional risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}). We also report,
rather than hide, an honest negative: under a fixed-threshold policy, global triage does not yet beat
direct diagnosis on confidence-gated sensitivity ($0.818$ for direct diagnosis versus $0.788$ for the
strongest variant at a $20\%$ referral budget), which we read as motivation for learned routing
thresholds. Code and benchmark will be released.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

\paragraph{Problem.}
Diagnostic models for skin lesions are typically trained and evaluated on curated dermoscopic
images, yet real consumer-device photographs span a wide quality range --- motion blur, poor
illumination, incomplete framing, colour casts, and low contrast. A model that is accurate on clean
data can be both wrong and confident on degraded data. The deployment question is therefore not only
``what is the diagnosis?'' but ``is this image good enough to diagnose, and if not, what should the
system do about it?''

\paragraph{Why existing tools fall short.}
Three families of methods each address part of the problem but none closes the loop.
(i) \emph{Post-hoc calibration} --- temperature scaling \citep{guo2017calibration}, Platt and isotonic
regression --- adjusts confidence after training but is quality-oblivious: it cannot tell apart
uncertainty from genuine ambiguity versus uncertainty from degradation.
(ii) \emph{Uncertainty estimation} via MC dropout \citep{gal2016dropout} or deep ensembles
\citep{lakshminarayanan2017simple} likewise produces quality-oblivious uncertainty and adds
inference cost.
(iii) \emph{Generic image enhancement} (e.g.\ Real-ESRGAN \citep{wang2021realesrgan}, Restormer
\citep{zamir2022restormer}) improves perceptual quality
with no guarantee that diagnostic content is preserved --- and generative restoration risks
inventing structures (pigment networks, vessels) that are clinically unacceptable.

\paragraph{Closed-loop quality-triage.}
We argue that quality must be a first-class signal threaded through the whole pipeline. Our system
forms a closed loop: \visiscore assesses quality; the agent decides whether to diagnose directly,
diagnose with caution, enhance-then-diagnose (\visienhance), or query for a retake; and the final
diagnosis is produced by a frozen classifier whose posterior confidence is the signal the gate routes
on (a standard bottleneck posterior suffices for this confidence, \S\ref{sec:exp-dca}). Crucially,
the quality scalar that drives this loop need not come from \visiscore alone --- it can be any
per-input quality estimate (a learned scorer, a no-reference IQA metric, or a known corruption
severity), which keeps the framework general.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
To motivate quality-aware triage, we first chart a reliability~$\times$~recoverability decision
surface (per-dimension~$\times$~per-severity, AUC-led, with a recoverability axis derived from
enhancement) that quantifies where degradation breaks diagnosis and where enhancement can recover it;
this observation, which complements rather than reuses prior per-dimension calibration views, frames
the three contributions that follow.
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{C1 --- Diagnosis-preserving enhancement with a mutual-information lower bound.} We
introduce a feature-level diagnosis-preserving objective (DP-Loss) under which, within a
moderate-degradation window, enhancement does not erode diagnostic content. We derive a
Pinsker-optimal mutual-information lower bound, $I(\Zenh;Y)\geq I(\Zref;Y)-\beta\sqrt{\epsilon}$ under
a KL budget $\Ldp\leq\epsilon$ (Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}), together with a directional entropy result
(Proposition~\ref{prop:entropy}), and confirm them empirically via the DP-Loss ablation
($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}$ improves by $+0.021$, $95\%$ CI $[+0.005,+0.035]$; McNemar
$p=2.3\times10^{-45}$).
\item \textbf{C2 --- A query-for-retake agent.} Our routing policy selects among four channels ---
direct diagnosis, cautioned diagnosis, enhance-then-diagnose, and query-for-retake --- where, to our
knowledge, the query-for-retake channel is the first to request \emph{re-acquisition} rather than
merely abstaining or deferring to a clinician. Its licence to enhance is backed by a local, per-band
conditional risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}): the enhancement benefit is positive only
inside a quality band $[\tauenh,\tauhigh]$, and the policy routes outside it to a retake.
\item \textbf{C3 --- An honest reliability boundary.} We show, and do not hide, where enhancement turns
net-negative: severely degraded malignant cases, where reweighting the reconstruction loss does not
close the salvage gap (a melanoma salvage of $5.2\%$ against a $31\%$ damage rate), and extreme
contrast loss at the per-dimension level. These boundaries are the hardest motivation for the
query-for-retake gate; correspondingly, under a fixed-threshold policy global triage does not yet beat
direct diagnosis (confidence-gated sensitivity $0.818$ versus $0.788$), which we report as motivation
for learned routing thresholds rather than a result to suppress.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
We presented a closed-loop quality-triage system for skin-lesion diagnosis on degraded consumer
imagery --- one that decides, per input, when to diagnose directly, when to apply
diagnosis-preserving enhancement, and when to request a retake rather than enhance blindly. The
constructive core is a recipe for enhancing \emph{safely}: a feature-level diagnosis-preserving
objective whose information loss is bounded by a mutual-information lower bound (Lemma~\ref{lem:dp},
realised empirically by the DP-Loss ablation), so that within a moderate-degradation window
enhancement does not erode diagnostic content. Around this recipe we build a query-for-retake
agent whose four-channel routing is governed by a local, per-band conditional risk guarantee
(Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}): enhancement is licensed only inside the band where it provably
helps, and re-acquisition is requested outside it.

We also chart, rather than hide, where this system reaches its limits. Enhancement turns net-negative
on the hardest cases --- extreme contrast loss at the per-dimension level
(\cref{sec:exp-surface}) and malignant lesions under severe degradation, where reweighting the
reconstruction loss fails to close the salvage gap (\cref{sec:exp-boundary}) --- which is exactly
the regime the retake channel is designed to divert. And under a fixed-threshold policy, global
triage does not yet beat direct diagnosis on net benefit (\cref{sec:exp-triage}); we make no claim
to the contrary, and we read this honest negative as the motivation for learned routing thresholds
rather than a result to suppress. Mapping where degradation-aware enhancement helps and where it
must yield to re-acquisition is, we believe, the more useful contribution for deploying
medical imaging on the quality-varying inputs that curated benchmarks exclude by design.

\section*{Ethics Statement}\label{sec:ethics}
This work uses only publicly available, de-identified dermatology datasets under their respective
licences; it does not recruit participants, collect new patient imagery, or run any prospective
clinical protocol. We make \emph{no} claim of clinician validation: the system is a research
framework, not a medical device, and production use would require institutional review, regulatory
clearance, and prospective trials (\cref{sec:discussion}). To mitigate dual-use and over-trust risks,
the agent includes an explicit refuse channel, enhancement is deterministic and diagnosis-preserving
rather than generative (avoiding hallucinated structure; \cref{sec:discussion}). Subgroup fairness
across sex, age, and skin tone is a recognised concern for deployment; a per-subgroup calibration
analysis is reported in companion work and is not claimed here. Clinical value is assessed only
through decision-curve analysis and triage simulation, each with
stated limits (Appendix~\ref{app:a20}); an LLM-as-judge audit is specified as a protocol
(Appendix~\ref{app:a19}) but its ratings are not yet collected and are deferred to the
camera-ready, under the disclaimer in Appendix~\ref{app:a23}.

\section*{Reproducibility Statement}\label{sec:repro}
All theoretical claims are stated with explicit assumptions and full proofs in
Appendices~\ref{app:a1}--\ref{app:a3}. The benchmark construction (sources, quality scoring,
