\section{Closed-Loop Quality-Triage Agent}\label{sec:agent}

A diagnosis-preserving restorer (\cref{sec:enhance}) is only safe to apply \emph{within} the
salvage band; outside it, enhancement either does nothing useful or, as our honest boundary
evidence shows (\cref{sec:exp-boundary}), actively harms the diagnosis. The role of the agent is
therefore not to diagnose, but to \emph{decide} --- per input, from a quality reading --- whether
to diagnose directly, to enhance and then diagnose, or to \emph{ask for a better image}. We make
the last channel explicit because it is, to our knowledge, what existing agentic and
selective-prediction pipelines do not provide.

\subsection{Two decision pathways behind one quality gate}\label{sec:agent-pathways}
The controller reads a per-input quality scalar $\qbar(x)$ --- learned here by \visiscore{}, but in
principle any per-input quality estimate (\eg a no-reference IQA score, or the corruption severity
when it is known a priori) --- and uses it as a gate that opens onto two qualitatively different
pathways:
\begin{itemize}
\item an \textbf{enhancement pathway}, taken when the image is degraded but \emph{recoverable}: the
quality-preserving restorer $\Tw$ is applied and diagnosis proceeds on the enhanced image, with the
local risk reduction certified (within the band) by \cref{thm:agent-compact};
\item a \textbf{query-for-retake pathway}, taken when the image is \emph{not} good enough to enhance
safely: rather than abstaining or deferring the case to a clinician, the agent issues a
\emph{re-acquisition request} --- it asks for the image to be captured again --- closing a
collection loop around the diagnostic model.
\end{itemize}
The second pathway is the exclusive hook of this work. Existing deferral systems return control
\emph{to a human} (defer-to-clinician) or simply withhold a prediction (abstain); the closest
agentic medical system we are aware of, AT-CXR \citep{atcxr2025}, routes chest-radiograph cases by
uncertainty but contains no quality gate and no re-acquisition channel, and selective prediction
\citep{geifman2017selective} only abstains. Teledermatology and ultrasound image-quality work does
flag or instruct re-capture from an image-quality classifier
\citep{vodrahalli2021teledermatology,jalaboi2023explainable,zhao2020lungus}, giving the clinical
motivation, but in each case the re-capture decision is decoupled from the diagnostic objective and
carries no accompanying risk analysis. We are therefore careful to scope our claim precisely: to
our knowledge this is the first acquisition closed loop that embeds re-acquisition \emph{inside a
risk-bounded routing policy}, gated by the same quality scalar that drives enhancement, coupled to
the diagnostic objective, and equipped with a local conditional risk guarantee
(\cref{sec:agent-thm2}) --- rather than a stand-alone quality flag that prompts re-photography.

\subsection{Four-channel routing policy}\label{sec:agent-routing}
Refining the two pathways with a secondary uncertainty check, the policy $\pi^{*}$ selects one of
four actions $\{\actd,\acte,\actq,\actr\}$ --- direct diagnosis, enhance-then-diagnose,
query-for-retake, and refuse --- from the quality scalar $\qbar$ and a predictive-entropy gate
$\hhigh$:
\begin{equation}
\pi^{*}(x)=
\begin{cases}
\actd & \qbar(x)\geq\tauhigh,\\[1pt]
\acte & \qbar(x)\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh]\ \text{and}\ \Hent(\hat{p}_x)\leq\hhigh,\\[1pt]
\actq & \taulow\leq\qbar(x)<\tauenh\ \text{or}\ \text{low completeness } q_3,\\[1pt]
\actr & \qbar(x)<\taulow\ \text{or}\ \big(\qbar(x)\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh]\ \text{and}\ \Hent(\hat{p}_x)>\hhigh\big),
\end{cases}
\label{eq:agent-policy-body}
\end{equation}
with ordered quality thresholds $\taulow<\tauenh<\tauhigh$ and the entropy gate $\hhigh$ set to a
high percentile of the validation predictive-entropy distribution. The entropy gate is what
distinguishes a \emph{cautioned} high-quality case (quality-sufficient yet intrinsically ambiguous,
which enhancement cannot salvage) from one that the enhancement pathway can help. Crucially, the
agent never emits a diagnosis on its own: each channel hands off to the same downstream classifier
on either the original image, the restored image, or a re-acquired image, and the agent's only
decision is \emph{which}. The uncertainty signal used by the gate is taken off the shelf (a standard
variational information bottleneck predictor already supplies a per-input entropy); the routing does
not depend on any bespoke quality-conditioned bottleneck.\footnote{An earlier
quality-conditioned variational bottleneck variant was evaluated as a candidate uncertainty source
but showed no measurable advantage over the standard variant for this purpose, so the policy relies
only on off-the-shelf uncertainty.}

\paragraph{Threshold selection.}
The thresholds $(\taulow,\tauenh,\tauhigh)$ are fixed by a validation grid search rather than
learned. The grid search yields three quality tiers, $\taulow=0.25$, $\tauenh=0.35$ and
$\tauhigh=0.50$: a \emph{severe} tier $\qbar<0.25$ on which re-acquisition is requested, a
\emph{moderate} (salvage) band $\qbar\in[0.35,0.50]$ over which enhancement is credited
(\cref{sec:enhance}), and a \emph{high}-quality tier $\qbar\geq0.50$ routed to direct diagnosis;
the intermediate stratum $\qbar\in[0.25,0.35)$ is the enhance-then-diagnose region. These tier
edges are the empirical band boundaries reported alongside the routing analysis in
\cref{sec:exp-dca}.

\subsection{A local, per-band risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact})}\label{sec:agent-thm2}
% R11/R2: LOCAL per-band only; explicitly deny global dominance; point honest negative to §7.7.
The enhancement pathway admits a conditional risk guarantee. \emph{Within} the salvage band, and
under a diagnosis-preserving restorer satisfying the information lower bound of
\cref{sec:enhance}~(Lemma), we derive that enhance-then-diagnose attains a strictly lower
conditional expected risk than direct diagnosis,
\begin{equation}
\Renh(x)\;\leq\;\Rdirect(x)-\Delta(\qbar(x),\Tw),
\qquad
\Delta(\qbar,\Tw)>0\ \ \text{for}\ \ \qbar\in[\tauenh,\tauhigh],
\label{eq:thm2-band}
\end{equation}
with the per-band reductions $\Delta(\qbar,\Tw)$ realised empirically, their bootstrap $95\%$
confidence intervals excluding zero in every band (\cref{sec:exp-boundary}). The compact statement
below records this guarantee; the full decision-theoretic setup, the four auxiliary lemmas, and the
finite-sample companion are deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:a2-thm2}.

This is deliberately a \emph{local} result, tied to the band condition: it certifies that
enhancement is the risk-reducing action exactly where the salvage band holds, and is silent
everywhere else. We make \textbf{no} claim that the routed agent dominates direct diagnosis
globally. The local within-band gain does not aggregate to a global ordering, for two concrete
reasons: the query channel's safety relies on a re-acquisition operator that improves quality in
expectation --- a premise that need not hold at deployment --- and the thresholds are fixed by grid
search rather than optimised against the deployed risk. Consistently with this, our own triage
simulation reports an honest \emph{negative} global result: at a fixed referral budget the globally
routed policy does \emph{not} outperform direct diagnosis (direct triage sensitivity $0.818$ versus
the routed agent's $0.788$ at a $20\%$ referral budget, with net benefit statistically
indistinguishable across direct diagnosis and all bottleneck variants --- overlapping $95\%$
bootstrap confidence intervals; \cref{sec:exp-dca}). We therefore present
Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact} as a local, conditional guarantee and treat the global utility of
the routed policy as an open, empirically negative question at present. Closing the gap between the
band-local gain and global utility --- by \emph{learning} the routing thresholds rather than fixing
them by grid search --- is left to future work. We further stress the scope of the guarantee with
respect to class: the band-local statement is a population (expected-risk) result over the
salvage-band subpopulation; it does \emph{not} certify a per-class risk reduction, and in
particular the malignant subgroup --- whose net-negative salvage is the hardest boundary evidence
(\cref{sec:exp-boundary}) --- remains within the query-for-retake regime rather than the enhance
regime (\cref{sec:agent-routing}, \cref{sec:exp-boundary}).

\subsection{Agent implementation}\label{sec:agent-impl}
The routing policy \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body} is realised as a lightweight ReAct-style controller
backed by a small language model with a deterministic rule fallback, so that the four-channel
decision is auditable and the thresholds $(\taulow,\tauenh,\tauhigh)$ are explicit rather than
implicit. The controller observes the per-dimension quality vector and the predictive entropy,
reasons over the policy in \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body}, and emits the chosen action together with a
short rationale; when the language model is unavailable or its output violates the threshold rules,
the deterministic fallback applies \eqref{eq:agent-policy-body} directly, guaranteeing that the
routing is never less safe than the rule. Full implementation details --- the controller prompt, the
rule-fallback specification, and a worked ReAct trace --- are given in Appendix~\ref{app:a5}.

The query-for-retake channel fires exactly where it should: it is highest on the severe degradation
band, where the policy routes a fraction $0.889$ ($95\%$ CI $[0.800, 0.978]$) of cases to
re-acquisition, falling to $0.651$ ($95\%$ CI $[0.600, 0.705]$) on the moderate band
(\cref{sec:exp-boundary}). The rate thus rises monotonically as quality falls, which is the behaviour
the gate is designed to produce rather than a property to claim away.
