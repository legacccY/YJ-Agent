\textbf{Main results on the \itb{} benchmark.} 
AUC and Expected Calibration Error (ECE, $M{=}15$ bins) on \itb-LQ ($n{=}300$) and \itb-HQ ($n{=}360$). 
\textbf{QCDI} $= \mathrm{ECE}_\mathrm{LQ} - \mathrm{ECE}_\mathrm{HQ}$ (closer to 0 = more quality-aware). 
$\rho(\!H,\bar q\!)$: Spearman correlation between predictive entropy and quality on the full \itb{} pool ($n{=}2{,}820$); more negative = more quality-aware. 
Subscripts: half-width of 1{,}000-iter bootstrap 95\% CI. 
\textbf{Bold}/\underline{underline} = best/second best per column. 
Cell shade is a per-column heat-map (red = worse, scaled to that column's range); the \textbf{\qvib{} Full (Ours)} row is highlighted. 
VIB-family models intentionally trade discriminative AUC for tighter, quality-aware calibration: the headline metrics are \textbf{QCDI} and $\rho$. Among trainable models, \qvib{} Full attains the best QCDI ($+0.006$) and a strongly negative $\rho$, the behaviour predicted by Prop.~2 (entropy monotone in quality); on aggregate ECE it ties Std VIB, but unlike Std VIB its calibration is quality-conditional. \qcts{} (a prior post-hoc method) is reported as a calibration ablation.
All $\rho$ values are statistically significant (Spearman two-sided, $p<0.01$; smallest $|\rho|$ at $p{=}1.7{\times}10^{-3}$). The $\rho$ difference between \qvib{} Full ($-0.192$) and the Adaptive-Prior ablation ($-0.191$) lies within the bootstrap CI and is not statistically distinguishable; \qvib{} Full's advantage rests on its quality-conditional guarantee (Prop.~2) and the best QCDI, not on a separable $\rho$.
$^\ast$\,\qvib{}+TokFT additionally fine-tunes the \qvib{} quality tokenizer with an auxiliary quality-regression loss; it is a supplementary \emph{discriminative} variant that trades calibration for a stronger entropy--quality coupling, and is not the primary model.
