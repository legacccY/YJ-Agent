\begin{abstract}
% LOCKED hook (anti-drift rule 1 / R14): constructive, closed-loop quality-triage.
% ACCV reframe (蓝图 §4.1): method/system paper 腔, DP-Enhance headline, 负结果如实压末尾, 数字全锁定值.
Skin-lesion diagnostic models trained on curated dermoscopy degrade sharply on real consumer-device
photographs --- blurred, poorly lit, incompletely framed, colour-cast, low-contrast --- on which a
model can be confidently wrong. Generic restoration improves perceptual quality but offers \emph{no
guarantee that diagnostic content survives}, and blindly deferring every imperfect image wastes
recoverable cases. We present \visienhance, a \emph{diagnosis-preserving enhancement} method wrapped in
a \emph{closed-loop quality-triage} system. Our core contribution is a feature-level
diagnosis-preserving objective (DP-Loss) whose information loss we bound by a Pinsker-optimal
mutual-information lower bound ($I(\Zenh;Y)\geq I(\Zref;Y)-\beta\sqrt{\epsilon}$, Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}).
Within a moderate-degradation window, enhancement leaves diagnosis essentially unchanged
($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.012$, $95.8\%$ enhanced-versus-reference agreement, McNemar $p=0.57$), and a
DP-Loss ablation confirms the effect is attributable to the objective ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}$
$+0.0205$, $95\%$ CI $[+0.005,+0.035]$; McNemar $p=2.3\times10^{-45}$); against six restoration
baselines \visienhance{} preserves diagnosis better on every one under a matched zero-shot protocol (a matched fine-tuned comparison is reported in \cref{sec:exp-transfer}). A per-input quality estimate (our
instantiation uses a five-head assessor, PLCC $0.924$; any no-reference scalar suffices) routes each
image to one of four channels --- diagnose, cautioned diagnose, enhance-then-diagnose, or \emph{query
for a retake} --- the last, to our knowledge, the \textbf{first re-acquisition channel} in this
setting, under a \emph{local, per-band} conditional risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}).
We \emph{report, rather than hide}, two honest boundaries that justify the retake gate: enhancement
turns net-negative on severely degraded melanoma (salvage $5.2\%$ against a $31\%$ damage rate,
unchanged under a mask-reweighted retrain), and under a fixed-threshold policy global triage does not
yet beat direct diagnosis (confidence-gated sensitivity $0.818$ versus $0.788$ at a $20\%$ referral
budget) --- motivating learned routing thresholds. Code and benchmark will be released.
\keywords{skin lesion diagnosis \and image quality assessment \and quality-triage \and
diagnosis-preserving enhancement \and uncertainty estimation}
\end{abstract}

\section*{Ethics Statement}\label{sec:ethics}
This work uses only publicly available, de-identified dermatology datasets under their respective
licences; it does not recruit participants, collect new patient imagery, or run any prospective
clinical protocol. We make \emph{no} claim of clinician validation: the system is a research
framework, not a medical device, and production use would require institutional review, regulatory
clearance, and prospective trials (\cref{sec:discussion}). To mitigate dual-use and over-trust risks,
the agent includes an explicit refuse channel, enhancement is deterministic and diagnosis-preserving
rather than generative (avoiding hallucinated structure; \cref{sec:discussion}). Subgroup fairness
across sex, age, and skin tone is a recognised concern for deployment; a per-subgroup calibration
analysis is reported in companion work and is not claimed here. Clinical value is assessed only
through decision-curve analysis and triage simulation, each with
stated limits (Appendix~\ref{app:a20}); an LLM-as-judge audit is specified as a protocol
(Appendix~\ref{app:a19}) but its ratings are not yet collected and are deferred to the
camera-ready, under the disclaimer in Appendix~\ref{app:a23}.

\section*{Reproducibility Statement}\label{sec:repro}
All theoretical claims are stated with explicit assumptions and full proofs in
Appendices~\ref{app:a1}--\ref{app:a3}. The benchmark construction (sources, quality scoring,
degradation tiers, subset definitions, composition) is detailed in Appendix~\ref{app:a4}, and the
complete environment, data splits, seeds, compute budget, and release artefacts --- including code,
configurations, checkpoints, and benchmark metadata --- are described in Appendix~\ref{app:a26}. A
one-command \texttt{reproduce.sh} regenerates the reported figures and statistics.
