\begin{abstract}
% LOCKED hook (anti-drift rule 1 / R14): constructive, closed-loop quality-triage.
We present a closed-loop quality-triage system for skin-lesion diagnosis on degraded consumer
imagery. Per input, it decides whether to diagnose directly, to apply diagnosis-preserving
enhancement, or to query for a retake, rather than enhancing every image or deferring blindly. The
decision is driven by a per-input quality estimate; our instantiation uses a five-head assessor
(\visiscore, PLCC $0.924$ / SRCC $0.895$), but any no-reference quality scalar suffices. Our first
contribution is a recipe for enhancing \emph{safely}: a feature-level diagnosis-preserving objective
(DP-Loss) whose information loss we bound by a Pinsker-optimal mutual-information lower bound
(Lemma~\ref{lem:dp}). Within a moderate-degradation window, enhancement leaves diagnosis essentially
unchanged ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.012$, $95.8\%$ enhanced-versus-reference agreement, McNemar
$p=0.57$), and the DP-Loss ablation confirms the effect ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}_{\text{enh}}$ improves by
$+0.0205$, $95\%$ CI $[+0.005,+0.035]$; McNemar $p=2.3\times10^{-45}$). To map \emph{when} enhancement
is licensed, we chart a reliability~$\times$~recoverability decision surface (per-dimension~$\times$~per-severity,
AUC-led): blur is the most fragile dimension ($\Delta\mathrm{AUC}=-0.091$ at the most severe setting),
and extreme contrast loss is the one regime where enhancement is significantly harmful ($-0.036$,
$95\%$ CI excluding zero). Outside the safe window the system requests a retake --- to our knowledge
the first re-acquisition channel in this setting, rather than mere abstention --- under a local,
per-band conditional risk guarantee (Theorem~\ref{thm:agent-compact}). We report, rather than hide, an
honest negative: under a fixed-threshold policy, global triage does not yet beat direct diagnosis on
confidence-gated sensitivity ($0.818$ versus $0.788$ for the strongest variant at a $20\%$ referral
budget), which motivates learned routing thresholds. Code and benchmark will be released.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Ethics Statement}\label{sec:ethics}
This work uses only publicly available, de-identified dermatology datasets under their respective
licences; it does not recruit participants, collect new patient imagery, or run any prospective
clinical protocol. We make \emph{no} claim of clinician validation: the system is a research
framework, not a medical device, and production use would require institutional review, regulatory
clearance, and prospective trials (\cref{sec:discussion}). To mitigate dual-use and over-trust risks,
the agent includes an explicit refuse channel, enhancement is deterministic and diagnosis-preserving
rather than generative (avoiding hallucinated structure; \cref{sec:discussion}). Subgroup fairness
across sex, age, and skin tone is a recognised concern for deployment; a per-subgroup calibration
analysis is reported in companion work and is not claimed here. Clinical value is assessed only
through decision-curve analysis and triage simulation, each with
stated limits (Appendix~\ref{app:a20}); an LLM-as-judge audit is specified as a protocol
(Appendix~\ref{app:a19}) but its ratings are not yet collected and are deferred to the
camera-ready, under the disclaimer in Appendix~\ref{app:a23}.

\section*{Reproducibility Statement}\label{sec:repro}
All theoretical claims are stated with explicit assumptions and full proofs in
Appendices~\ref{app:a1}--\ref{app:a3}. The benchmark construction (sources, quality scoring,
degradation tiers, subset definitions, composition) is detailed in Appendix~\ref{app:a4}, and the
complete environment, data splits, seeds, compute budget, and release artefacts --- including code,
configurations, checkpoints, and benchmark metadata --- are described in Appendix~\ref{app:a26}. A
one-command \texttt{reproduce.sh} regenerates the reported figures and statistics.
